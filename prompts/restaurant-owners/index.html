15 ChatGPT Prompts for Restaurant Owners That Fill Tables

Running a restaurant means managing operations, staff, and marketing simultaneously. These prompts handle the marketing writing so you can focus on food, service, and guests.

Prompt 1 of 15
Menu item description (appetizing copy)
Write appetizing menu descriptions for these dishes: [LIST 3-5 DISHES with key ingredients]. For each: start with a vivid sensory word, mention the key ingredients (2-3), note the cooking method or origin, and end with a taste/texture note. Under 30 words each. No generic words like 'delicious' or 'amazing'. Evoke the experience.
💡 Pro tip: Menus with well-written item descriptions increase average check size by 5-8%. Word choice drives orders.
Prompt 2 of 15
Google review response (positive)
Write a response to this positive Google review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Thank the guest by name, reference something specific they mentioned, share something they might not know about the restaurant ([FACT/UPCOMING EVENT]), and invite them back. Under 100 words. Warm, personal — not a template.
💡 Pro tip: Responding to positive reviews increases the likelihood of that reviewer returning by 30%.
Prompt 3 of 15
Google review response (negative)
Write a professional response to this negative Google review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Acknowledge the experience without being defensive, apologize for falling short, invite them to contact you directly to make it right ([EMAIL/PHONE]). Do NOT make excuses or argue. Under 100 words. Defuse, don't escalate.
💡 Pro tip: How you handle negative reviews is your biggest public relations moment. Other readers are evaluating you, not just the original reviewer.
Prompt 4 of 15
Email campaign (special event/promotion)
Write an email campaign for [EVENT/PROMOTION — e.g., Valentine's Day dinner, Happy Hour launch, new menu reveal]. Include: subject line (creates curiosity or urgency), 2-sentence hook about why this event is special, what's offered (details), reservation/booking CTA with link, and event date/time. Under 200 words. Warm and enticing.
💡 Pro tip: Email campaigns for special events sent 7 days and 2 days before the event maximize reservation rates.
Prompt 5 of 15
Instagram caption (food photo)
Write 3 Instagram caption variations for a food photo of [DISH NAME] at [RESTAURANT NAME]. Version 1: atmospheric/storytelling. Version 2: ingredient-focused (what makes this dish special). Version 3: engagement hook (question to followers). Each under 150 words. Include 5 relevant hashtags at the end.
💡 Pro tip: Rotating between storytelling, educational, and engagement captions keeps your feed from feeling repetitive.
Prompt 6 of 15
Staff job posting (FOH/BOH)
Write a job posting for a [SERVER/LINE COOK/HOST/BARISTA] at [RESTAURANT NAME], a [CUISINE TYPE] restaurant in [CITY]. Include: what makes working here different from other restaurants, responsibilities (5 bullets), requirements (3-5 must-haves), compensation ([HOURLY/TIP POLICY]), and how to apply. Under 300 words. Tone: welcoming but clear on expectations.
💡 Pro tip: Job postings that describe the team culture and growth opportunities attract better applicants than those focused only on requirements.
Prompt 7 of 15
Loyalty program launch email
Write an email to existing customers announcing the launch of [RESTAURANT NAME]'s loyalty program. Explain: how it works (earn/redeem), what they get for signing up now ([BONUS]), and how to join. Keep it simple — people won't read complicated points systems. Under 200 words. CTA: sign up link or QR code mention.
💡 Pro tip: Loyalty programs increase visit frequency by 40% in the first year. The launch email is your biggest enrollment opportunity.
Prompt 8 of 15
Seasonal menu launch social post
Write a social media post announcing the launch of our new [SEASON] menu at [RESTAURANT NAME]. Highlight 2-3 standout dishes with brief descriptions. Include: launch date, a CTA to make a reservation, and a behind-the-scenes detail (chef inspiration, local ingredient source). Platform: [Instagram/Facebook]. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Seasonal menu launches create natural urgency ('limited time') that drives reservations without discounting.
Prompt 9 of 15
Response to food delivery app complaint
Write a response to this complaint on [DoorDash/Uber Eats/Yelp]: '[PASTE COMPLAINT]'. Acknowledge the issue, apologize without blame-shifting, explain what you've done or will do to fix it ([SPECIFIC ACTION]), and offer a resolution if appropriate ([CREDIT/REPLACEMENT]). Professional, brief. Under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Delivery app responses are public. A calm, solution-oriented response converts complaints into retained customers.
Prompt 10 of 15
Private dining / event inquiry response
Write a response to an inquiry about booking [RESTAURANT NAME] for a private event. The inquiry: [PASTE INQUIRY or describe: 'birthday dinner for 20']. Include: confirmation of interest, key information they need (capacity, minimum spend, menu options), what happens next (call to customize), and your contact details. Under 200 words. Professional, welcoming.
💡 Pro tip: Private events are 3-5x the revenue of regular covers. Respond within 2 hours to capture the booking.
Prompt 11 of 15
Weekly specials post (social media)
Write a social media post announcing this week's specials at [RESTAURANT NAME]: [LIST SPECIALS]. Create appetite with specific, sensory language. Include days/times available and a CTA to visit or make a reservation. Under 150 words. Pair with a note to post with a food photo.
💡 Pro tip: Consistent weekly specials posts train your followers to check in every week — building a habit around your brand.
Prompt 12 of 15
Influencer outreach (food blogger/Instagrammer)
Write an outreach email to food influencer [NAME] ([@HANDLE], [X] followers) inviting them to dine at [RESTAURANT NAME]. Mention something specific about their content that aligns with your restaurant ([DETAIL]). Offer a complimentary dinner for them and a guest on a mutually convenient date. No strings attached — just let them share their honest experience. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Remove the 'must post' requirement. Influencers who visit freely and authentically produce better content — and their followers can tell the difference.
Prompt 13 of 15
Takeaway packaging insert (brand message)
Write the copy for a small card/insert included in takeaway orders from [RESTAURANT NAME]. Include: a warm thank-you message, a brief brand story (2 sentences), a CTA to follow on Instagram ([HANDLE]) and leave a review ([LINK/QR]), and maybe a returning customer offer. Under 100 words. Personal, not corporate.
💡 Pro tip: Packaging inserts are read by 85% of people who receive them — it's the most-read branded content you produce.
Prompt 14 of 15
Staff training reminder memo
Write a brief staff memo reminding the team about [STANDARD — e.g., table turn time, upselling specials, greeting script]. Cover: why it matters (customer experience + revenue), the specific expectation, and one example of doing it right. Under 150 words. Positive, team-oriented tone — not punitive.
💡 Pro tip: Positive reinforcement memos (celebrating what good looks like) outperform disciplinary reminders for standards compliance.
Prompt 15 of 15
OpenTable / Resy listing description
Write a restaurant listing description for [RESTAURANT NAME] for OpenTable or Resy. Cover: cuisine type, atmosphere/vibe, signature dishes (2-3), neighborhood, price range, and what makes this place worth booking in advance. Under 200 words. Enticing but accurate.
💡 Pro tip: Reservation platform descriptions that describe the atmosphere and signature dishes convert browsers to bookings at 2x the rate of generic descriptions.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Content Writing That Actually Work

Most ChatGPT content writing prompts produce generic output. These prompts are engineered to produce content that sounds human, matches your brand voice, and ranks in search engines.

Prompt 1 of 5
Blog post outline (full structure)
Write a detailed blog post outline for "[TOPIC]" targeting the keyword "[KEYWORD]". Include: a hook-driven introduction, 5-7 H2 headings with 2-3 H3 subpoints each, a data/stat section, a common objections section, and a conclusion with CTA. Target length: 1,800 words. Audience: [AUDIENCE].
💡 Pro tip: Replace [TOPIC], [KEYWORD], and [AUDIENCE] with your specifics. The more precise you are, the less editing you'll need.
Prompt 2 of 5
Introduction paragraph (hook formula)
Write 3 different introduction paragraphs for a blog post about "[TOPIC]". Use a different hook for each: (1) a surprising statistic, (2) a relatable problem/pain point, (3) a bold contrarian claim. Each intro should be 60-80 words and end with a sentence that makes the reader want to read on.
💡 Pro tip: Test all 3 intros. The contrarian version usually performs best on social shares.
Prompt 3 of 5
Headline generator (10 variations)
Generate 10 headline variations for a blog post about "[TOPIC]". Include: 2 how-to headlines, 2 list headlines, 2 question headlines, 2 benefit-driven headlines, and 2 curiosity-gap headlines. Target keyword: "[KEYWORD]". Keep all under 65 characters for SEO.
💡 Pro tip: Use Headline Analyzer to score each one. Aim for 70+ score before publishing.
Prompt 4 of 5
Conclusion with CTA
Write a conclusion for a blog post about "[TOPIC]". The conclusion should: (1) summarize the 3 most important takeaways in 2 sentences each, (2) reinforce the main benefit, (3) end with a clear CTA that links to "[YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]". Tone: [TONE]. Keep it under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Strong conclusions reduce bounce rate significantly. The CTA placement here is one of the highest-converting spots.
Prompt 5 of 5
SEO meta description
Write 3 meta descriptions for a blog post titled "[TITLE]". Each must: be under 155 characters, include the keyword "[KEYWORD]", have a clear benefit or curiosity hook, and end with a subtle CTA. Number them 1-3.
💡 Pro tip: Meta descriptions directly affect CTR. Test 2 versions in Google Search Console.

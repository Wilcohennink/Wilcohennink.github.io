15 ChatGPT Prompts for Financial Advisors That Build Trust and Win Clients

Financial advisors who educate consistently attract better clients. These prompts help you create newsletters, social content, prospect emails, and meeting prep materials — without spending hours writing from scratch.

Prompt 1 of 15
Client newsletter (market update)
Write a monthly client newsletter section about current market conditions as of [MONTH/YEAR]. Include: a 2-sentence summary of what happened in markets, what it means for long-term investors (calm, educational tone), and one actionable thought or question for clients to reflect on. Under 250 words. No specific security recommendations. Add a compliance disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Market commentary that reduces anxiety builds more trust than any performance update.
Prompt 2 of 15
LinkedIn educational post
Write a LinkedIn post explaining [FINANCIAL CONCEPT — e.g., dollar-cost averaging, sequence of returns risk, Roth conversion]. Structure: 1-line hook that challenges a common assumption, 3-4 short paragraphs with a simple analogy, and a key takeaway. Under 1,200 characters. No jargon. End with a question to drive comments. Add compliance disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Advisors who post weekly educational content on LinkedIn report 3x more referral inquiries.
Prompt 3 of 15
Prospect follow-up email (after intro meeting)
Write a follow-up email to prospect [NAME] after an initial discovery meeting. Thank them for their time, summarize what you learned about their goals ([GOALS]), outline the proposed next step ([NEXT STEP]), and set a clear timeline. Under 200 words. Tone: warm, organized, low-pressure.
💡 Pro tip: Same-day follow-up emails (sent within 2 hours) have 40% higher response rates.
Prompt 4 of 15
Retirement planning explainer (client-facing)
Write a client-friendly explainer on [RETIREMENT TOPIC — e.g., the 4% rule, RMDs, Social Security timing]. Cover: what it is in simple terms, why it matters for their plan, common mistakes to avoid, and when to revisit this decision. Under 300 words. No specific advice — educational only. Add disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Proactively educating clients on topics before they ask reduces anxiety and increases retention.
Prompt 5 of 15
Annual review meeting agenda email
Write an email to client [NAME] ahead of their annual review meeting on [DATE]. Include: meeting agenda (3-4 items), a short list of questions to think about before the call (3 questions), and a reminder to bring any life changes (job, family, property) to discuss. Under 200 words. Tone: organized, warm.
💡 Pro tip: Clients who receive a pre-meeting agenda arrive more prepared and feel more value from the review.
Prompt 6 of 15
Referral request email (to existing client)
Write an email to client [NAME] asking for a referral. Thank them for being a valued client, explain that my practice grows through word-of-mouth, and make a specific ask: 'If you have a colleague, family member, or friend who might benefit from a financial planning conversation, I'd love an introduction.' Keep it short and non-transactional. Under 120 words.
💡 Pro tip: The best time to ask is right after a positive event — a great investment return, a plan milestone, or a positive review call.
Prompt 7 of 15
Risk tolerance explanation email
Write an email to client [NAME] explaining the concept of risk tolerance after completing their risk assessment. Explain: what their score means ([CONSERVATIVE/MODERATE/AGGRESSIVE]), how it shapes their portfolio, and why we review it periodically. Make it feel personalized, not generic. Under 200 words. Add compliance disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Clients who understand their risk tolerance are less likely to panic-sell during market downturns.
Prompt 8 of 15
Cold outreach email (to HNW prospect)
Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME], a [PROFESSION] at [COMPANY/CITY] who fits my ideal client profile ([DEMOGRAPHIC]). Lead with a relevant insight or question about their financial situation (not a pitch). Offer a free, specific resource ([RESOURCE — e.g., tax planning checklist]). CTA: 15-minute discovery call. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Cold emails that lead with a question about the prospect's situation get 3x more replies than product-led emails.
Prompt 9 of 15
Social Security timing explainer
Write a 300-word educational piece on when to claim Social Security for clients aged 60-65. Cover the three main claiming windows (62, full retirement age, 70), the break-even analysis, and the most overlooked factor ([HEALTH/MARITAL STATUS/INCOME NEEDS]). Plain English, no jargon. Educational only — not personalized advice. Add disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Social Security timing is one of the most common questions after age 60. An evergreen explainer drives SEO traffic year-round.
Prompt 10 of 15
Tax-loss harvesting explainer (Q4)
Write a Q4 client email explaining tax-loss harvesting. Cover: what it is, who benefits, the wash-sale rule (simplified), and the deadline. Encourage clients to reach out to discuss their specific situation. Under 250 words. Educational only, not advice. Add compliance disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Proactive Q4 tax emails generate more client conversations than any other seasonal communication.
Prompt 11 of 15
Onboarding welcome email (new client)
Write a welcome email to new client [NAME] who just signed on. Include: a warm welcome, what they can expect in the first 30 days (3 milestones), how to reach me/my team, and an expression of your commitment to their goals. Under 200 words. Tone: warm, organized, reassuring.
💡 Pro tip: The onboarding experience sets the tone for the entire relationship. First impressions in financial services take years to undo.
Prompt 12 of 15
Estate planning trigger email
Write an email to existing clients introducing estate planning as a topic they may want to address. Cover: 3 life events that make estate planning urgent (marriage, new child, property purchase, business ownership), what a basic estate plan includes, and a CTA to schedule a conversation. Educational, not sales-y. Under 200 words. Add disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Estate planning discussions deepen client relationships and often expand AUM through asset consolidation.
Prompt 13 of 15
Objection handler: 'I already have an advisor'
Write a response to a prospect who says 'I already have a financial advisor.' Acknowledge respectfully, ask a genuine question about their current experience ([QUESTION — e.g., 'When did you last have a full financial plan review?']), and position a second opinion as a low-risk option. Do not badmouth their current advisor. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: A second-opinion approach converts 30% of 'already have an advisor' prospects over time.
Prompt 14 of 15
Fee transparency explainer (how we get paid)
Write a client-facing explanation of how [YOUR FIRM NAME] is compensated. Cover: fee structure ([FEE-ONLY/AUM/COMMISSION]), what clients pay, what they receive for that fee, and why this structure aligns our incentives with theirs. Honest, clear, no fine print. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Transparent fee conversations reduce churn — clients who understand how you're paid stay longer.
Prompt 15 of 15
Webinar invitation email
Write an invitation email to clients and prospects for a free webinar on '[TOPIC — e.g., Retirement Planning After 55]' on [DATE/TIME]. Cover: what they'll learn (3 specific takeaways), who should attend, and a clear CTA to register via [LINK]. Create light urgency (limited spots or live Q&A). Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Financial advisors who run regular webinars convert 2x more cold leads into clients vs. those who don't.

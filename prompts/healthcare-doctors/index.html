15 ChatGPT Prompts for Healthcare Professionals That Improve Patient Communication

Healthcare professionals who communicate clearly see better patient outcomes, higher satisfaction scores, and stronger practices. These prompts help you write everything from patient education materials to practice marketing — always with accuracy and compliance in mind.

Prompt 1 of 15
Patient education handout (condition explainer)
Write a patient education handout about [CONDITION/PROCEDURE] for patients with a 6th-grade reading level. Include: what it is (plain language definition), common causes or risk factors, key symptoms to watch for, treatment options (general overview), and when to call the doctor. Under 300 words. Add a note: 'Always discuss your specific situation with your healthcare provider.'
💡 Pro tip: Patient handouts written at a 6th-grade reading level improve comprehension rates by 40% — patients follow instructions better when they understand them.
Prompt 2 of 15
Appointment reminder message (SMS/email)
Write an appointment reminder for a patient's upcoming visit at [PRACTICE NAME] on [DATE] at [TIME] with [PROVIDER NAME]. Include: what to bring (insurance card, ID, medication list), parking or arrival instructions, how to confirm or cancel, and a friendly reminder to arrive 15 minutes early. SMS version (under 160 chars) and email version (under 150 words).
💡 Pro tip: Appointment reminder SMS messages sent 48 hours before the appointment reduce no-show rates by 30-50%.
Prompt 3 of 15
Practice website 'About Us' page
Write an 'About Us' page for [PRACTICE NAME], a [SPECIALTY] practice in [CITY]. Include: practice founding story (brief), mission statement, clinical approach (patient-centered language), team overview (placeholder for photos/bios), insurances accepted ([LIST or 'most major insurers']), and a CTA to book an appointment. Under 350 words. Warm, professional, trust-building.
💡 Pro tip: Healthcare practice About pages that express a specific patient care philosophy attract patients who are a better fit — and who stay longer.
Prompt 4 of 15
Google review request message
Write a brief message to send to a patient (via email or SMS) requesting a Google review for [PRACTICE NAME]. Thank them for being a patient, explain that reviews help other patients find quality care, and include the direct Google review link. Under 75 words. HIPAA-note: no specific health information, consent assumed via opt-in.
💡 Pro tip: Healthcare practices with 20+ Google reviews appear in local 'near me' searches at significantly higher rates. Each review is worth roughly 3 new patient inquiries per year.
Prompt 5 of 15
Post-visit follow-up message
Write a post-visit follow-up message for [PRACTICE NAME] to send to patients 2 days after a [PROCEDURE/APPOINTMENT TYPE]. Check in on how they're feeling, remind them of key post-visit instructions ([INSTRUCTIONS — e.g., rest, medication schedule, follow-up timing]), and let them know how to reach the office with questions. Under 150 words. Caring, clear.
💡 Pro tip: Post-visit follow-up messages improve patient satisfaction scores significantly and reduce anxious 'just checking' calls to your office.
Prompt 6 of 15
New patient welcome email
Write a welcome email for new patients of [PRACTICE NAME]. Include: warm welcome from the provider, what to expect at their first appointment (what to bring, how long, what will happen), how to access the patient portal, office contact information, and a brief note on the practice's approach to care. Under 200 words. Reassuring, organized.
💡 Pro tip: New patient welcome emails reduce first-appointment anxiety and improve completion of pre-visit paperwork by 35%.
Prompt 7 of 15
LinkedIn post (healthcare professional)
Write a LinkedIn post for [DR./PROVIDER NAME] who specializes in [SPECIALTY] about [HEALTHCARE TOPIC — clinical insight, practice change, patient care lesson]. Audience: fellow healthcare professionals, potential patients, healthcare industry contacts. Structure: 1-line insight hook, 2-3 paragraphs with a specific example (no patient information), and a key takeaway. Under 1,200 characters. Professional but human.
💡 Pro tip: Healthcare professionals who share specific clinical learnings (with patient privacy protected) build the most credible LinkedIn followings in their specialty.
Prompt 8 of 15
Informed consent explanation email
Write a patient-friendly email explaining the informed consent process for [PROCEDURE] at [PRACTICE NAME]. Cover: what the procedure involves (brief, plain language), the benefits (why we recommend it), the main risks ([GENERAL RISKS]), alternatives if they choose not to proceed, and the next steps (where to sign, timeline). Under 250 words. Educational only — actual consent is obtained in clinic. Add disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Pre-procedure educational emails reduce patient anxiety and the number of last-minute cancellations due to fear of the unknown.
Prompt 9 of 15
Practice announcement (new service or provider)
Write a practice announcement email to existing patients at [PRACTICE NAME] announcing [NEW SERVICE/PROCEDURE/PROVIDER]. Include: what's new and why we added it, who will benefit from it, how to book, and any introductory offer if applicable. Under 200 words. Professional, patient-benefit focused.
💡 Pro tip: Practice announcement emails sent to existing patients generate more new service bookings than any external marketing channel — your current patients are your best referral source.
Prompt 10 of 15
Referral thank-you letter (to referring physician)
Write a professional thank-you letter to [DR. NAME] at [PRACTICE/HOSPITAL] for referring patient [GENERAL DESCRIPTION, no patient name] for [SPECIALTY/CONDITION TYPE]. Confirm you've seen the patient, briefly describe your management approach (general), and express appreciation for the ongoing referral relationship. Under 150 words. Professional, collegial.
💡 Pro tip: Referring physicians who receive timely, informative thank-you letters for referrals are 4x more likely to refer again.
Prompt 11 of 15
Medical blog post (patient-facing)
Write a patient-facing blog post on [MEDICAL TOPIC] for [PRACTICE NAME]'s website. Target audience: [PATIENT DEMOGRAPHIC]. Include: a headline targeting a common question ([TARGET KEYWORD]), an accessible explanation of the topic, 3-4 practical takeaways patients can act on, and a CTA to book an appointment if symptoms persist. Under 500 words. Add disclaimer: this is educational content, not medical advice.
💡 Pro tip: Medical blog posts that answer the top questions patients Google for your specialty drive the highest-quality new patient inquiries from local search.
Prompt 12 of 15
Telehealth appointment instructions email
Write an email to a patient preparing for their upcoming telehealth appointment with [PROVIDER NAME] at [PRACTICE NAME] on [DATE] at [TIME]. Include: tech requirements and setup instructions, how to access the appointment link, what to have ready (current medication list, recent test results), and a troubleshooting contact if they have tech problems. Under 200 words. Clear, low-anxiety.
💡 Pro tip: Telehealth preparation emails reduce appointment technical issues by 60% and show rates by 25%.
Prompt 13 of 15
Patient satisfaction survey request
Write a brief patient satisfaction survey request for [PRACTICE NAME] to send after appointments. Explain why feedback matters (improving care quality), keep the survey to 3-5 questions, include the survey link, and assure patients their responses are anonymous and used constructively. Under 100 words. HIPAA-compliant framing (no health info in the email).
💡 Pro tip: Short patient surveys (under 5 questions) have 5x higher completion rates than comprehensive satisfaction instruments.
Prompt 14 of 15
After-hours voicemail script
Write an after-hours voicemail script for [PRACTICE NAME]. Include: name and hours of the practice, instructions for medical emergencies (call 911 / go to ED), option to leave a message for non-urgent matters, and when they can expect a callback. Under 100 words. Clear, calm, reassuring.
💡 Pro tip: After-hours voicemail scripts with specific emergency instructions reduce patients bypassing the ED for genuinely urgent matters.
Prompt 15 of 15
Case presentation abstract (conference submission)
Write a case presentation abstract for a [MEDICAL SPECIALTY] case study to submit to [CONFERENCE NAME]. Include: case introduction (patient demographics, chief complaint), key clinical findings, differential diagnosis considerations, management approach, outcome, and clinical learning points. Under 250 words. Standard academic abstract format. Note: remove all PHI before submission.
💡 Pro tip: Case abstracts that lead with the unusual or educational element of the case (not just the chronology) are selected for conference presentation more often.

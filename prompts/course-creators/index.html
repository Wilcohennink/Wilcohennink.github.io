15 ChatGPT Prompts for Course Creators That Fill Your Course and Grow Your Revenue

Creating a course is 20% of the work. Marketing it is the other 80%. These prompts help you write the sales pages, launch emails, and content that fill your course — and create lesson materials that get students real results.

Prompt 1 of 15
Course sales page (full structure)
Write a sales page structure for online course '[COURSE NAME]' teaching [TOPIC]. Target student: [AVATAR]. Include sections: (1) Hero (headline + sub-headline + CTA), (2) The problem (before state), (3) Why existing solutions fail, (4) Who you are (credibility), (5) What's inside (module breakdown), (6) Student results (placeholder testimonials), (7) FAQ (5 questions), (8) Price and offer, (9) Guarantee, (10) Final CTA. Each section: 50-150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Sales pages that include 'why other solutions fail' convert 2x better — you're validating why the student has struggled before finding you.
Prompt 2 of 15
Course launch email sequence (5 emails)
Write a 5-email launch sequence for '[COURSE NAME]' opening on [LAUNCH DATE]. Email 1: Problem + story. Email 2: The solution framework. Email 3: Student success story (placeholder). Email 4: Open cart announcement. Email 5: Last chance (cart closes [DATE]). Each under 250 words. Builds desire before the pitch.
💡 Pro tip: Launch sequences that wait until Email 4 to reveal the offer convert 3x better than sequences that pitch from Email 1.
Prompt 3 of 15
Module overview script (lesson intro)
Write an intro script for Module [NUMBER] of [COURSE NAME] titled '[MODULE TITLE]'. Cover: what the student has already accomplished (previous module recap), what this module will cover, why it matters for their end goal, and a 'by the end of this module you will...' statement with 3 specific outcomes. Under 200 words. Energetic, teacher voice.
💡 Pro tip: Module intros that recap progress and preview outcomes keep students motivated through the 'messy middle' of a course.
Prompt 4 of 15
Webinar script (free training / lead-gen)
Write a 45-minute free training script for [TOPIC] that leads into an offer for '[COURSE NAME]'. Structure: 5-min intro (story + credibility), 30-min teaching (3 key frameworks or insights), 10-min transition to the offer (bridge from content to course). Teaching should provide genuine value that makes attendees think: 'If this is the free stuff, the paid course must be incredible.'
💡 Pro tip: Webinars that over-deliver on free value have the highest conversion rates. Teach your best stuff — it creates proof of your expertise.
Prompt 5 of 15
YouTube video script (course promotion)
Write a 10-minute YouTube video script about '[VIDEO TOPIC]' that naturally promotes [COURSE NAME]. Structure: 0-1 min: hook (who this is for and what they'll learn). 1-8 min: teaching (3-5 actionable points). 8-9 min: bridge to the course ('if you want the full system...'). 9-10 min: CTA and outro. SEO keyword: '[KEYWORD]'.
💡 Pro tip: YouTube videos that teach a complete, useful concept (not just teasers) rank better and convert viewers to course buyers more reliably.
Prompt 6 of 15
Testimonial collection message (student outreach)
Write a message to a course student [NAME] who has completed [COURSE NAME] and had good results. Ask for a testimonial. Provide 3 specific questions: (1) What was your situation before taking the course? (2) What specific result have you achieved? (3) Who would you recommend this course to and why? Offer to write a draft from their answers if easier. Under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Specific questions produce 10x better testimonials than 'would you mind leaving a review'. Students want to help — they just don't know what to say.
Prompt 7 of 15
Email to non-buyers post-launch (nurture)
Write an email to people who were on the launch list but didn't buy '[COURSE NAME]'. Acknowledge the launch has closed. Provide one piece of genuine value related to the course topic. Let them know when (or if) doors will open again, and invite them to join the waitlist. No pressure. Under 150 words. Gracious, not desperate.
💡 Pro tip: Post-launch nurture emails keep non-buyers warm for your next launch cycle. 30% of eventual course buyers don't purchase on their first exposure.
Prompt 8 of 15
Course outline / curriculum design
Design a curriculum outline for an online course on '[TOPIC]' aimed at [STUDENT LEVEL — beginner/intermediate]. The course should take students from [STARTING POINT] to [END OUTCOME] in [NUMBER] weeks. Structure: [NUMBER] modules, each with 3-5 lessons and one action exercise. Include a pre-course quick win (lesson they complete in the first hour) and a final capstone project. Include lesson titles for each module.
💡 Pro tip: Course curriculums that include a quick win in Module 1 reduce early drop-off dramatically — students who complete the first lesson are 3x more likely to finish the course.
Prompt 9 of 15
Podcast / interview promotional copy
Write promo copy for an appearance by [COURSE CREATOR NAME] on '[PODCAST NAME]' (or vice versa) where they discuss [TOPIC]. Format: (1) Social post (host shares), (2) Email newsletter feature (50 words), (3) Instagram Story text overlay (3 slides). Each promotes the episode with a specific hook and links to listen.
💡 Pro tip: Podcast appearances are the highest-quality leads for course creators — listeners know, like, and trust you after 30-60 minutes before they've seen your sales page.
Prompt 10 of 15
Student welcome email (post-enrollment)
Write a welcome email to a student who just enrolled in '[COURSE NAME]'. Include: excitement and congratulations, a reminder of the transformation they're working toward, how to access the course, what to do in the first 24 hours (one specific action), and how to reach you with questions. Under 200 words. Warm, motivating, organized.
💡 Pro tip: The welcome email sets the tone for the entire learning relationship. Students who start strong (complete the first lesson within 24 hours) complete the course at 5x the rate.
Prompt 11 of 15
Free mini-course email sequence (lead gen)
Write a 3-email free mini-course sequence on '[MINI TOPIC]' as a lead magnet for '[PAID COURSE NAME]'. Email 1 (Day 0): Lesson 1 (the most impactful insight). Email 2 (Day 2): Lesson 2 (a practical technique). Email 3 (Day 4): Lesson 3 + bridge to the paid course. Each lesson under 400 words. Deliver massive value, then make the offer feel obvious.
💡 Pro tip: Free mini-courses that deliver a complete outcome (not just teasers) convert to paid course sales at 8-12% — the highest lead magnet conversion rate.
Prompt 12 of 15
Refund policy and objection handling
Write a refund policy section for [COURSE NAME] sales page and 3 FAQ answers that handle common objections: (1) 'I've tried courses before and they didn't work.' (2) 'I don't have time right now.' (3) 'Is this right for my specific situation ([SITUATION])?' Keep refund policy clear and specific. FAQ answers under 100 words each. Confident, empathetic.
💡 Pro tip: Objection handling on a sales page reduces cart abandonment significantly. Address the top 3 hesitations before they become reasons not to buy.
Prompt 13 of 15
Affiliate program invitation email
Write an email inviting a [CONTENT CREATOR/PODCASTER/BLOGGER] with an audience of [AVATAR] to become an affiliate for [COURSE NAME]. Cover: what the course is, who it's for, the affiliate commission ([%] or [$]), support you provide (swipe copy, banners, tracking), and payout terms. CTA: link to apply or reply to discuss. Under 200 words. Value-forward.
💡 Pro tip: Affiliate programs with high commissions (30-50%) and ready-made swipe copy attract the best affiliate partners. Remove all friction from their decision.
Prompt 14 of 15
Course waitlist page copy
Write copy for a course waitlist landing page for '[COURSE NAME]' (currently closed). Include: a headline (the transformation, not the course name), 3 bullet points of what students will achieve, a brief 'who this is for' section, a strong reason to join the waitlist (waitlist-only bonus or early access), and email opt-in CTA. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: Waitlist pages with a waitlist-only bonus (early bird pricing, bonus module) convert at 2x the rate of plain 'notify me' pages.
Prompt 15 of 15
Behind-the-scenes Instagram / TikTok script
Write a behind-the-scenes social media post or short video script (30-60 seconds) showing the creation process of [COURSE NAME]. Options: recording a module, designing slide materials, working with a student, or reviewing curriculum. Include the hook, 3 quick insights from behind the scenes, and a soft CTA (follow for updates / join the waitlist). Authentic, process-focused.
💡 Pro tip: Behind-the-scenes content about course creation builds anticipation and trust simultaneously — future students feel invested before the course even launches.

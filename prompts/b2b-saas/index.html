15 ChatGPT Prompts for B2B SaaS That Drive MRR

B2B SaaS growth comes from the same 4 levers: acquire, activate, retain, expand. These prompts cover every lever — from cold outreach and landing pages to onboarding flows and expansion emails.

Prompt 1 of 15
Cold email (ICP outreach)
Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME], a [TITLE] at [COMPANY TYPE], for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME]. Lead with a specific pain point relevant to their role ([PAIN POINT]). Describe the outcome we deliver in 1 sentence. Offer a low-friction CTA (15-min demo or specific resource). Under 125 words. No product features — outcome-first.
💡 Pro tip: B2B cold emails that lead with a specific, role-relevant pain point get 3x more replies than those leading with product features.
Prompt 2 of 15
Trial activation email sequence (3 emails)
Write a 3-email trial activation sequence for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME]. Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + one key action to take today (the 'aha moment' trigger). Email 2 (Day 3): Contextual tip based on what most trial users haven't done yet ([FEATURE]). Email 3 (Day 7): Value reinforcement + upgrade CTA with the main benefit. Each under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Trial emails that guide users to the 'aha moment' increase trial-to-paid conversion by 30-50%.
Prompt 3 of 15
Product landing page copy (core value prop)
Write landing page copy for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME] targeting [ICP]. Include: hero headline (outcome-focused, under 10 words), sub-headline (who it's for + key benefit, under 20 words), problem section (the pain without your product, 2-3 sentences), solution section (how you solve it), 3 feature → benefit pairs, social proof placeholder (testimonials, logos), and primary CTA button text. Under 500 words.
💡 Pro tip: SaaS landing pages that lead with outcomes ('Close deals 40% faster') convert 2x better than those leading with features ('Automated pipeline management').
Prompt 4 of 15
Feature announcement email (to existing users)
Write a feature announcement email to existing users of [SAAS PRODUCT] announcing [NEW FEATURE NAME]. Structure: subject line (benefit-led), 1-sentence hook, what the feature does, why we built it (user feedback reference), how to access it, and a link to the full announcement or documentation. Under 200 words. Celebratory but brief.
💡 Pro tip: Feature announcements that reference user feedback ('You asked for this') generate the highest engagement of any product email type.
Prompt 5 of 15
Churn survey email
Write an email to [SAAS PRODUCT] customers who cancelled or are about to cancel. Ask for feedback on why they're leaving. Keep the survey to 2 questions maximum: (1) What's the main reason you're cancelling? (multiple choice with 5 options + other) and (2) What would it take to bring you back? Under 100 words. No sales language.
💡 Pro tip: Single-question churn surveys have 3x the response rate of 5+ question surveys. Prioritize the most valuable insight.
Prompt 6 of 15
Demo follow-up email
Write a follow-up email to [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY] after a product demo of [SAAS PRODUCT]. Summarize what we covered, address 1-2 questions or concerns raised during the call ([CONCERNS]), include relevant case study or social proof, and propose clear next steps ([TRIAL/PROPOSAL/ANOTHER CALL]). Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Demo follow-up emails that reference specific concerns raised during the call convert at 2x the rate of generic follow-ups.
Prompt 7 of 15
Pricing page copy
Write pricing page copy for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME] with [2/3] tiers: [TIER NAMES]. For each tier: name, price, 1-sentence positioning, 5 key features/inclusions, and ideal customer description. Include a comparison table header row. Add a FAQ section with 3 questions: (1) Can I change plans? (2) Is there a free trial? (3) What happens when I hit the limit? Under 500 words.
💡 Pro tip: Pricing pages with 'ideal for' descriptions for each tier reduce sales team questions and increase self-serve conversion.
Prompt 8 of 15
G2/Capterra review request email
Write an in-app or email request for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME] customers to leave a G2 or Capterra review. Explain the value of reviews for the community (not just you). Make the ask specific and low-friction (direct link, 2-3 minutes). Send at a moment of success ([AFTER MILESTONE/POSITIVE INTERACTION]). Under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Review request timing matters more than copy. The moment right after a user achieves their goal is peak satisfaction.
Prompt 9 of 15
Investor update email (for SaaS startup)
Write a monthly investor update for [SAAS STARTUP NAME]. Include: key metrics (MRR [$X], MoM growth [%], churn [%], new customers [N]), highlights (3 bullets), challenges (honest, 1-2), capital runway ([MONTHS]), and asks (specific 2-3 requests for intro/advice). Under 400 words. Transparent, no spin.
💡 Pro tip: Investor updates that include honest challenges and specific asks generate more value per email than pure highlight reports.
Prompt 10 of 15
Partnership outreach (technology partner)
Write an email to [PARTNER COMPANY NAME] proposing a technology integration partnership. Our product: [YOUR PRODUCT]. Their product: [THEIR PRODUCT]. Overlap: [SHARED ICP]. The integration idea: [DESCRIPTION]. Mutual benefits: [LIST]. CTA: 30-minute call to explore. Under 200 words. Value-first, specific.
💡 Pro tip: Partnership pitches that quantify the mutual benefit ('combined, we reach 10,000 [ICP] companies') get 3x more responses.
Prompt 11 of 15
Case study outline (SaaS customer win)
Write a case study outline for [CUSTOMER COMPANY NAME] using [SAAS PRODUCT]. Include sections: (1) Customer background (2 sentences), (2) The challenge before [PRODUCT], (3) Why they chose us over alternatives, (4) How they implemented it, (5) Results (specific metrics: [METRICS]), (6) What they'd tell other [ICP TYPE] companies. 200-250 words. Quote placeholder included.
💡 Pro tip: SaaS case studies that include the competitor they evaluated (and why they chose you) convert high-intent readers 2x better.
Prompt 12 of 15
Webinar promotion email
Write a promotion email for a SaaS webinar titled '[WEBINAR TITLE]' on [DATE]. Target: [AUDIENCE — prospects / customers / both]. Include: subject line, 1-line hook, 3 specific learnings from the webinar, speaker credentials (1 sentence each for up to 2 speakers), logistics, and registration CTA with link. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Webinar emails that promise 3 specific learnings (not 'insights and best practices') convert at 2x the rate of vague event invitations.
Prompt 13 of 15
Expansion email (to existing customer)
Write an expansion email to customer [COMPANY NAME] proposing an upgrade from [CURRENT PLAN] to [HIGHER PLAN]. Lead with their current usage data ([USAGE METRIC — near the limit, specific feature they'd benefit from]). Frame the upgrade as enabling their growth. Include the price difference and key additional benefits. CTA: 15-min call. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Expansion emails that reference specific usage data ('you've used 90% of your API calls this month') convert at 5x the rate of generic upgrade pitches.
Prompt 14 of 15
Email signature for SDR/AE (with social proof)
Write a professional email signature for [SDR/AE NAME] at [SAAS COMPANY]. Include: name, title, company, phone, LinkedIn URL, and 1 line of social proof (customer count, notable customer logo, award, or a link to a case study). Compact — under 5 lines. Multiple format options: minimal, social-proof-heavy, resource-link.
💡 Pro tip: Email signatures with one social proof element (customer logo or review rating) increase reply rates by 8-12% in B2B outreach.
Prompt 15 of 15
Help article (product documentation)
Write a help article for [SAAS PRODUCT NAME] explaining how to [DO FEATURE/TASK]. Include: a 1-sentence overview, prerequisites (if any), step-by-step instructions (numbered), a tip for the most common mistake, and a 'what's next' section linking to related articles. Target audience: new users. Under 400 words. Clear, scannable.
💡 Pro tip: Help articles with numbered steps and a 'common mistake' section reduce support ticket volume by 20-30% for that feature.

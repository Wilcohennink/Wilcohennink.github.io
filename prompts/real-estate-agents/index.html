15 ChatGPT Prompts for Real Estate Agents That Win More Listings

Real estate is a relationship business, but relationships start with the right words. These prompts help you write listing descriptions that sell, emails that get responses, and social posts that build your local authority.

Prompt 1 of 15
Listing description (residential)
Write a compelling MLS listing description for a [BEDROOMS]-bed, [BATHROOMS]-bath [PROPERTY TYPE] in [NEIGHBORHOOD/CITY]. Key features: [LIST 5 FEATURES — e.g., renovated kitchen, pool, corner lot]. Price: [PRICE]. Highlight the lifestyle, not just the specs. Lead with the strongest selling point. Under 200 words. No clichés like "cozy" or "charming".
💡 Pro tip: Lead with the one feature buyers can't get elsewhere in the neighborhood.
Prompt 2 of 15
Property teaser (social media)
Write a social media post announcing this new listing: [PROPERTY ADDRESS/DESCRIPTION]. Platform: [Instagram/Facebook/LinkedIn]. Include: a scroll-stopping first line, 3 key features as bullet points, price (if public), and a clear CTA to DM or click the link. Tone: excited but professional. Add 5 relevant hashtags.
💡 Pro tip: Post within 24 hours of going live — first-day engagement drives algorithm reach.
Prompt 3 of 15
Buyer follow-up email (after showing)
Write a follow-up email to [BUYER NAME] after showing them [PROPERTY ADDRESS] on [DATE]. Acknowledge what they liked and any concerns they raised ([CONCERNS]). Position the home's strengths. Include a soft CTA to schedule a second viewing or discuss an offer. Under 150 words. No pressure. Tone: helpful advisor.
💡 Pro tip: Send within 2 hours of the showing while the home is fresh in their mind.
Prompt 4 of 15
Expired listing outreach letter
Write an outreach letter to the owner of an expired listing at [ADDRESS]. Their listing expired after [DAYS] days with [PREVIOUS AGENT/BROKERAGE]. Acknowledge their frustration without badmouthing competitors. Explain what I do differently: [YOUR DIFFERENTIATOR]. Include a specific offer (free market analysis, updated pricing strategy). CTA: 15-minute call. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Expired listings are warm leads — owners want to sell and are frustrated. Lead with empathy.
Prompt 5 of 15
Seller CMA presentation narrative
Write a narrative summary for a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) presentation for [SELLER NAME] at [ADDRESS]. The analysis shows: recommended list price [PRICE], 3 comparable sales [COMP DETAILS], average days on market [DOM], and current absorption rate [RATE]. Explain why the recommended price maximizes both speed and net proceeds. Professional but conversational tone. 250 words.
💡 Pro tip: The narrative bridges the data gap — most sellers can't interpret raw comps without context.
Prompt 6 of 15
Open house follow-up sequence (3 emails)
Write a 3-email follow-up sequence for open house visitors at [ADDRESS]. Email 1 (Day 1): Thank them, include the listing link and key specs. Email 2 (Day 3): Share one fact about the neighborhood/market that adds urgency (e.g., inventory levels, recent comparable sale). Email 3 (Day 7): Last chance nudge + offer a private showing. Each under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Most agents send one email. Three touches triple your second-showing conversion rate.
Prompt 7 of 15
Neighborhood market update post
Write a [monthly/quarterly] neighborhood market update post for [NEIGHBORHOOD NAME] to share on social media and email. Include: average sale price ([PRICE]), days on market ([DOM]), list-to-sale ratio ([RATIO]), and 1-sentence insight on buyer vs. seller conditions. End with a CTA to contact me for a personalized home valuation. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Consistent market updates position you as the local expert — post monthly, not just when you have a listing.
Prompt 8 of 15
Objection handler: 'I want to wait until spring'
Write a response to a seller who says 'I want to wait until spring to list.' Acknowledge the instinct without dismissing it. Provide 2-3 data-driven reasons why listing now (or soon) may be advantageous in the current market ([MARKET CONDITIONS]). Offer to run a net proceeds comparison: list now vs. wait. Keep it under 150 words. Tone: consultative, not pushy.
💡 Pro tip: Always respond with data, not opinion. Sellers who wait are often leaving money on the table.
Prompt 9 of 15
Referral request email (past client)
Write an email to a past client [CLIENT NAME] asking for a referral. Remind them of what we accomplished together (sold [ADDRESS] for [RESULT]). Mention that my business grows through referrals. Make the ask specific: 'If you know anyone thinking of buying or selling in [AREA] in the next 6 months, I'd love an introduction.' Offer something in return ([VALUE — e.g., coffee, market report]). Under 120 words.
💡 Pro tip: A specific ask ('someone thinking of buying in the next 6 months') gets 4x more referrals than a vague one.
Prompt 10 of 15
Just-sold announcement post
Write a just-sold announcement for [ADDRESS] to post on social media. Include: final sale price vs. list price, days on market, and a one-sentence story about the seller or buyer's journey (generic, no names). End with a CTA for anyone thinking of selling in [NEIGHBORHOOD]. Tone: celebratory but professional. Add 3-5 hashtags.
💡 Pro tip: Just-sold posts are the most credible social proof you have. Post every single one.
Prompt 11 of 15
Video script (60-second listing tour)
Write a 60-second video script for a listing tour of [ADDRESS]. Structure: 10-sec hook (most compelling feature), 30-sec walkthrough highlights (5 features, 6 seconds each), 10-sec neighborhood/lifestyle line, 10-sec CTA. Conversational, camera-friendly language. No technical jargon.
💡 Pro tip: Video listings get 403% more inquiries than non-video. Shoot on your phone — authenticity beats production value.
Prompt 12 of 15
Pre-listing email to sphere of influence
Write an email to my sphere of influence (past clients, friends, network) announcing an upcoming listing at [ADDRESS] before it hits the MLS. Create urgency (coming to market [DATE]). Include: key details, price range, and an exclusive pre-market showing offer. CTA: reply to schedule a private preview. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Pre-market emails to your sphere often generate buyers before you spend a dollar on marketing.
Prompt 13 of 15
Google Business Profile post
Write a Google Business Profile post announcing [recent achievement: new listing / just sold / market update]. Include the address or neighborhood, a key stat, and a CTA to call or message for more info. Under 150 words. Keyword-rich but natural — include 'real estate agent [CITY]' and '[NEIGHBORHOOD] homes for sale' naturally.
💡 Pro tip: Google Business posts improve local search visibility. Post at least twice a month.
Prompt 14 of 15
Cold outreach to FSBO owner
Write a letter/email to a FSBO (For Sale By Owner) homeowner at [ADDRESS]. Acknowledge their desire to save the commission. Explain what an agent actually delivers: [YOUR VALUE POINTS — pricing data, marketing reach, negotiation]. Offer a no-obligation consultation. Do NOT push hard — make it easy to say yes to a conversation. Under 175 words.
💡 Pro tip: FSBO owners convert at 25% when approached with empathy, not a hard sell.
Prompt 15 of 15
Buyer consultation prep email
Write a pre-consultation email to a first-time buyer [BUYER NAME] preparing them for our buyer consultation meeting on [DATE]. Cover: what we'll discuss (their must-haves, market reality, process overview), what to bring (pre-approval or financial readiness), and a link to [YOUR CALENDAR/ZOOM LINK]. Set expectations warmly. Under 175 words. Tone: welcoming, expert.
💡 Pro tip: A prepared buyer respects your time and converts to an offer faster.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Startup Founders That Move the Needle

Founders wear a hundred hats. These prompts handle the writing so you can focus on building. From investor decks to product launch emails to team hiring, every template here is built for speed.

Prompt 1 of 15
Elevator pitch (30-second investor version)
Write a 30-second elevator pitch for my startup [COMPANY NAME]. We [WHAT WE DO] for [TARGET CUSTOMER] who struggle with [PROBLEM]. Unlike [ALTERNATIVE SOLUTION], we [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]. We've achieved [TRACTION — users, revenue, growth rate]. We're raising [AMOUNT] to [USE OF FUNDS]. Generate 3 variations: bold/direct, story-led, and problem-first.
💡 Pro tip: The best pitches make the investor feel the problem before you present the solution.
Prompt 2 of 15
Investor cold email (pre-seed/seed)
Write a cold email to investor [NAME] at [FUND] who invests in [STAGE/SECTOR]. Lead with a specific reason they're relevant (portfolio company, thesis match, recent tweet/article). Include: what we do in 1 sentence, traction in 1-2 numbers, the ask (15-min call or deck request). Under 150 words. No attachments in first email.
💡 Pro tip: The best investor cold emails reference something specific about the investor's thesis — it proves you did homework.
Prompt 3 of 15
Product Hunt launch copy
Write the Product Hunt launch copy for [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: tagline (under 60 chars), description (under 260 chars), and a 400-word 'maker comment' that covers the problem it solves, how it works, why we built it, who it's for, and an invitation to try it free at [URL]. Casual, founder voice, authentic.
💡 Pro tip: Maker comments that share the origin story of why you built this consistently outperform feature-focused descriptions.
Prompt 4 of 15
Investor update email (monthly/quarterly)
Write a monthly investor update email for [COMPANY NAME]. Include sections: Highlights (3 bullets, top wins), Metrics (MRR [AMT], growth [%], churn [%], users [N]), Focus for next month (3 priorities), Asks (specific help needed from investors — introductions, hires, advice), and a brief morale note. Under 400 words. Honest, not spin.
💡 Pro tip: Investors who receive consistent updates respond faster when you need help. Transparency builds trust.
Prompt 5 of 15
Job description (early startup hire)
Write a job description for our first [ROLE] hire at [COMPANY NAME], a [STAGE] startup in [INDUSTRY]. Frame it as a founder-adjacent role where they'll have outsized impact. Lead with the mission and what they'll own, not the perks. Required: [3-5 MUST-HAVES]. Be honest about the stage and what's hard. Under 350 words.
💡 Pro tip: Early hire descriptions that are honest about the challenges attract mission-driven candidates who stay.
Prompt 6 of 15
Landing page headline (A/B test set)
Write 5 landing page headline variations for [PRODUCT/STARTUP] targeting [TARGET CUSTOMER]. Each headline should be under 10 words and lead with a specific benefit or outcome. Include: 1 problem-focused, 1 outcome-focused, 1 time-to-value focused, 1 social proof-based, 1 contrarian/bold. Then pick the strongest and explain why.
💡 Pro tip: Headlines are the highest-leverage copy on any landing page. Test at least 3 before scaling ad spend.
Prompt 7 of 15
Launch announcement email (beta users)
Write an email to our beta user list announcing the official launch of [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: what's new since beta, a personal thank-you to early testers, a launch offer for beta users ([OFFER]), and a CTA to share with their network. Founder voice — authentic, excited, specific. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: Beta users are your best launch amplifiers. Give them a reason to shout about it on launch day.
Prompt 8 of 15
Press release (product launch / funding)
Write a press release announcing [PRODUCT LAUNCH / FUNDING ROUND] for [COMPANY NAME]. Include: headline, dateline, lead paragraph (who, what, where, when, why), 2 supporting paragraphs, a founder quote, a traction stat, investor quote (if funding), and boilerplate 'About' section. Standard AP style. 350-400 words.
💡 Pro tip: Press releases with a strong stat in the first paragraph get picked up 3x more often by journalists.
Prompt 9 of 15
Competitive differentiation one-pager
Write a competitive differentiation brief for [PRODUCT NAME] vs. [COMPETITOR 1] and [COMPETITOR 2]. Format as a comparison table: 5 dimensions (price, ease of use, [3 SPECIFIC FEATURES]), with a written summary (100 words) of our core differentiation that's defensible and honest. For sales team and investor deck use.
💡 Pro tip: Differentiation that admits competitor strengths is more credible than one that dismisses them.
Prompt 10 of 15
Demo request follow-up email
Write a follow-up email to [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY] who requested a product demo. Confirm the meeting details, share a 1-paragraph context on what the demo will cover, include a link to a relevant case study or quick video, and set expectations for the next step after the demo. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Pre-demo content (a relevant case study or 2-min video) improves show-up rates by 25%.
Prompt 11 of 15
Co-founder intro email (to potential co-founder)
Write an intro email to a potential co-founder [NAME] who has the skills I need ([SKILLS]). Reference how I found them or know them ([CONTEXT]). Describe the opportunity in terms of the problem being solved and the market size, not the features. Ask for a coffee chat or video call. Under 150 words. Vision-led, direct.
💡 Pro tip: Lead with the mission, not the equity or role title. Co-founders join for the problem, not the perks.
Prompt 12 of 15
VC cold intro through warm referral
Write a message to [MUTUAL CONTACT NAME] asking them to intro me to [VC NAME] at [FUND]. Include: brief context on my startup ([1 sentence]), why I specifically want to connect with this investor ([THESIS FIT]), and a forwardable blurb (50 words) they can include in the intro. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: A forwardable blurb removes all friction from your referral's intro. Make it easy and they'll do it.
Prompt 13 of 15
Content strategy (founder's LinkedIn)
Build a content strategy for my LinkedIn presence as the founder of [COMPANY] in [INDUSTRY]. Suggest: 4 content pillars (each with 3 post ideas), posting frequency, the tone I should use, and the one type of post that will grow followers fastest in my niche. My audience: [TARGET — investors / customers / potential hires].
💡 Pro tip: Founder content on LinkedIn drives recruiting, fundraising, and inbound sales simultaneously — the highest-leverage marketing channel for early-stage.
Prompt 14 of 15
Board meeting narrative (CEO update)
Write the CEO narrative section of a board deck for [COMPANY NAME]. Cover: state of the business (2-3 sentences), key wins since last meeting, key challenges (honest), metrics vs. goals (MRR [AMT] vs. target [TARGET]), and strategic focus for the next quarter. 300 words. Direct, no spin.
💡 Pro tip: Boards that receive honest narratives (including challenges) make better decisions than those fed optimistic spin.
Prompt 15 of 15
Waitlist launch email sequence
Write a 3-email waitlist launch sequence for [PRODUCT NAME]. Email 1: Announce the product and the waitlist, create intrigue with the core promise. Email 2 (Day 3): Give a behind-the-scenes look at what you're building. Email 3 (Day 7): Early access announcement — top of waitlist gets in first, CTA to share for priority access. Each under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Waitlist sequences with a 'move up' incentive (share to get earlier access) can 3x your launch list size.

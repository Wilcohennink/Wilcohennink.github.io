15 ChatGPT Prompts for LinkedIn That Grow Your Audience and Influence

LinkedIn rewards consistent, specific, and opinionated content. These prompts give you the formats that generate the most reach, engagement, and business impact — whether you're building a following from scratch or scaling an existing one.

Prompt 1 of 15
Contrarian opinion post (gets engagement)
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a contrarian opinion about [COMMON BELIEF IN MY INDUSTRY]. Structure: 1-line hook that challenges the belief, 3 short paragraphs building the argument with real examples, and a final reframe of the better approach. Under 1,300 characters. End with a question to spark comments.
💡 Pro tip: Contrarian posts generate 3x more comments than agreement posts. The algorithm rewards engagement, not approval.
Prompt 2 of 15
Personal story post (vulnerability + lesson)
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a professional lesson learned from a personal failure or challenge: [BRIEF CONTEXT OF CHALLENGE]. Structure: the situation (2-3 lines), what I did wrong or misunderstood, the turning point, the lesson I took away, and how I apply it now. Under 1,300 characters. Raw and honest, not self-deprecating.
💡 Pro tip: Vulnerability posts on LinkedIn generate the highest save-rate of any format — people save them to share later or revisit.
Prompt 3 of 15
Tactical list post (high save rate)
Write a LinkedIn list post with [X] things [AUDIENCE] needs to know about [TOPIC]. Format: intro line that teases the value, numbered list (each item is 1-3 lines with a bold statement + brief explanation), and a closing line with a question or call to share. Under 1,500 characters. Actionable, specific, no filler.
💡 Pro tip: List posts are the most-saved format on LinkedIn. Each item should be specific enough to screenshot and use immediately.
Prompt 4 of 15
Industry insight post (establishes authority)
Write a LinkedIn post sharing an insight about [INDUSTRY TREND OR DATA POINT]. Structure: stat or observation as a hook, 2-3 paragraphs of your unique interpretation (not just repeating the data), and a clear 'what this means for [AUDIENCE]' section. Under 1,300 characters. Cite your source in the first line.
💡 Pro tip: Insight posts that interpret data (not just share it) position you as an analyst, not just a commentator.
Prompt 5 of 15
Newsletter promotion post (grow subscribers)
Write a LinkedIn post promoting my newsletter '[NEWSLETTER NAME]' on [TOPIC]. Tease the content of the latest issue (3 specific insights or frameworks, no spoilers). Explain who it's for and why it's worth subscribing. CTA: link in comments or bio. Under 1,200 characters. Feels like value, not a pitch.
💡 Pro tip: Newsletter promo posts that reveal 2-3 specific insights (without giving them all away) convert followers to subscribers 4x better than generic 'subscribe here' posts.
Prompt 6 of 15
Hiring post (attract top talent)
Write a LinkedIn hiring post for a [ROLE] position at [COMPANY]. Lead with the mission and impact of the role (not the job title). Describe what great looks like (not just requirements). Explain what's unique about your team or culture. Include a direct apply link or CTA to DM. Under 800 characters. Attract, don't screen.
💡 Pro tip: Hiring posts that describe the problem the role will solve attract 2x better candidates than those that lead with job requirements.
Prompt 7 of 15
Comment reply strategy (engage top posts)
Write 5 thoughtful comment templates for engaging on LinkedIn posts in [MY NICHE]. Each comment should: add a new perspective or data point, be 2-4 sentences long, reference something specific from the original post, and end with a question or insight that adds to the conversation. Not generic praise. Real engagement.
💡 Pro tip: Commenting on top posts in your niche drives more profile views than most original posts. High-quality comments get noticed.
Prompt 8 of 15
Event/webinar announcement post
Write a LinkedIn post announcing [EVENT NAME] on [DATE]. Cover: who it's for (specific audience), what they'll learn (3 specific outcomes), format and duration, and a CTA to register via [LINK]. Create genuine urgency (limited seats or live Q&A). Under 1,000 characters.
💡 Pro tip: Event announcement posts with 3 specific learning outcomes get 3x more registrations than 'join us for...' posts.
Prompt 9 of 15
Creator behind-the-scenes post
Write a LinkedIn post sharing behind-the-scenes of [A PROJECT/CONTENT/BUSINESS INITIATIVE I'M WORKING ON]. Include: what you're building, why, one challenge you've run into, and one thing you've learned. Invite others to ask questions or share similar experiences. Under 1,200 characters. Authentic process content.
💡 Pro tip: Behind-the-scenes content builds parasocial connection — followers feel invested in your journey and are more likely to support your work.
Prompt 10 of 15
Polarizing question post (drive comments)
Write a LinkedIn post posing a polarizing question about [TOPIC] that has no obvious right answer. Frame it as a genuine debate worth having. Briefly share your position in 2-3 lines, then invite the audience to weigh in. Under 800 characters. Designed to generate comments, not likes.
💡 Pro tip: Debate posts that show the poster's own position (not just neutral questions) generate 50% more comments.
Prompt 11 of 15
Reshare + commentary post
I want to reshare [ARTICLE/POST/STAT ABOUT TOPIC] with my own perspective. Write the commentary text for the reshare (100-150 characters for the post text). My angle: [MY OPINION/INTERPRETATION]. Should add genuine value to what the original source said — not just 'great read'.
💡 Pro tip: Reshares with substantive commentary (not 'great post!') are treated as original content by the LinkedIn algorithm.
Prompt 12 of 15
Monthly review / reflection post
Write a LinkedIn post reflecting on [MONTH] at [COMPANY/PROJECT]. Include: 1 major win, 1 lesson from a failure or setback, 1 thing I'm looking forward to next month. Specific, honest, and brief — 3 short sections, under 1,000 characters total. Show the real work, not a highlight reel.
💡 Pro tip: Monthly review posts build a documented public journey — followers become invested in your progress over time.
Prompt 13 of 15
Post announcing a new role or milestone
Write a LinkedIn post announcing [NEW JOB/PROMOTION/BUSINESS MILESTONE] for [NAME]. Include: the news (1 line), what this role/milestone means personally, a thank-you to 2-3 key people (without being sycophantic), and what I'm focused on next. Under 1,000 characters. Gracious, excited, not self-congratulatory.
💡 Pro tip: Milestone posts that thank specific people and look forward (not just backward) resonate more than pure self-celebration.
Prompt 14 of 15
Outreach message (to new connection)
Write a LinkedIn connection request message to [PROSPECT/COLLABORATOR NAME] who is a [TITLE] at [COMPANY]. Reference a specific piece of their content or work ([DETAIL]). Mention a genuine reason for connecting (not a pitch). Under 200 characters (connection request limit).
💡 Pro tip: Never pitch in the connection request. The goal is the connection — the conversation comes after.
Prompt 15 of 15
Viral hook rewrite (improve your existing post)
Here is my LinkedIn post draft: [PASTE YOUR DRAFT]. Rewrite the first 2 lines as 5 alternative hook options that are more likely to stop the scroll. Keep the body of the post the same. Hook types to try: curiosity gap, bold claim, surprising stat, relatable pain point, and counterintuitive statement.
💡 Pro tip: The first 2 lines are the only thing 80% of people read before deciding to click 'see more'. Everything else is secondary.

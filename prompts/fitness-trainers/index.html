15 ChatGPT Prompts for Fitness Trainers That Build Your Business

The best trainers build results for their clients and a sustainable business for themselves. These prompts handle everything from workout program templates to Instagram captions to client retention emails — so you can stay focused on coaching.

Prompt 1 of 15
Custom workout program (4-week)
Design a 4-week workout program for a client with the following profile: Goal: [FAT LOSS/MUSCLE GAIN/ENDURANCE], Fitness level: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE], Available days: [X days/week], Equipment: [GYM/HOME/MINIMAL]. Structure each week with [X] training days and [X] rest days. Include sets, reps, and rest periods. Note progression from Week 1 to Week 4.
💡 Pro tip: Programs with built-in progression (increasing load or volume each week) show visible results faster, improving retention.
Prompt 2 of 15
Client check-in message (weekly)
Write a weekly check-in message to send to online client [NAME] at the start of week [NUMBER] of their program. Ask about: energy levels, adherence to workouts, nutrition wins/struggles, sleep quality, and any blockers. Keep it conversational and encouraging, not interrogative. Under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Consistent check-ins are the #1 driver of online client retention — even more than the program quality.
Prompt 3 of 15
Instagram caption (transformation post)
Write an Instagram caption for a client transformation post (before/after or progress photo, with permission). Include: context (weeks in program, what they worked on), 2-3 specific habits that created the result (not 'hard work' — be specific), and a CTA for followers who want similar results. 3-5 relevant hashtags. No before/after shaming language.
💡 Pro tip: Specific habit callouts ('she prioritized protein every meal and showed up 4x/week for 12 weeks') are more inspiring — and more trustworthy — than vague motivational language.
Prompt 4 of 15
Nutrition guide intro (macros explained)
Write a client-friendly introduction to macro tracking for a fitness client who has never counted macros. Cover: what macros are, why they matter (in context of their goal: [GOAL]), a simple starting estimate for their targets based on [WEIGHT/GOAL], and 2 practical tips for tracking without obsession. Under 250 words. Plain language, no dietitian-speak.
💡 Pro tip: Clients who understand why they're tracking macros are 3x more likely to stick to it.
Prompt 5 of 15
Sales page (online coaching program)
Write a sales page for my [PROGRAM NAME] online coaching program targeting [TARGET CLIENT — e.g., busy moms, men over 40]. Include: headline (outcome-focused), the problem they face, why what they've tried before didn't work, what's included in the program, social proof (2 testimonial placeholders), price, FAQ (3 questions), and a strong CTA. Under 600 words.
💡 Pro tip: The 'why what they've tried before didn't work' section is the most powerful sales conversion element on fitness pages.
Prompt 6 of 15
Email to re-engage inactive client
Write an email to a former client [NAME] who stopped training [TIMEFRAME] ago. Acknowledge the time gap without guilt-tripping. Share something relevant (a quick tip, a new program, or a client win that might inspire them). Invite them back with a low-barrier offer ([FREE SESSION/CALL/DISCOUNT]). Under 150 words. Warm, zero pressure.
💡 Pro tip: Former clients convert at 5x the rate of cold leads. A warm reconnect email is the highest-ROI client acquisition you can do.
Prompt 7 of 15
Free consultation script (gym floor / DM)
Write a 5-minute script for a free fitness consultation, either in person or via DM. Cover: 3 key qualifying questions, how to present my assessment, how to describe my coaching offer in terms of their specific goal, and how to close on a trial session or starting package. Natural, caring, not salesy.
💡 Pro tip: The best consultations feel like a coach listening to understand — not a salesperson trying to close.
Prompt 8 of 15
30-day challenge post (launch)
Write a social media post launching a [FREE / PAID] 30-day [CHALLENGE NAME] fitness challenge. Include: who it's for, what participants will do (3 bullet points), what they'll achieve by Day 30, how to join ([LINK / DM], and urgency: [START DATE or SPOTS LIMITED]. Under 300 words. Energetic but specific.
💡 Pro tip: Challenges with a specific outcome promise ('lose X lbs' or 'do your first pull-up') attract more sign-ups than vague ones.
Prompt 9 of 15
Gym introduction email (new member)
Write a welcome email to a new gym member [NAME] who just joined [GYM NAME]. Include: a warm welcome, a brief overview of resources available (classes, equipment, staff), an invitation to book a complimentary orientation session, and how to reach the team. Under 200 words. Welcoming and organized.
💡 Pro tip: New members who book an orientation in the first week are 50% more likely to still be members at 6 months.
Prompt 10 of 15
Testimonial request (end of program)
Write a message to send to a client who just completed a [PROGRAM/MILESTONE]. Ask for a short testimonial. Provide 3 prompts: (1) what was your challenge before starting, (2) what result did you achieve, (3) who would you recommend this to. Keep the ask under 100 words. Offer to put the words together for them if they prefer.
💡 Pro tip: Offering to draft the testimonial based on their answers removes the biggest friction — the blank page.
Prompt 11 of 15
TikTok/Reel hook (workout demo)
Write 5 video hook options for a fitness TikTok or Instagram Reel demonstrating [EXERCISE/TOPIC]. Each hook is the first line spoken to camera. Options should include: 1 myth-busting hook, 1 results-based hook, 1 relatable humor hook, 1 mistake-correction hook, 1 challenge hook. Under 10 words each.
💡 Pro tip: The first 2 seconds determine if the video gets watched. A strong hook doubles average watch time.
Prompt 12 of 15
New service announcement (group training)
Write a social post and short email announcing the launch of a new [GROUP TRAINING / BOOTCAMP / ONLINE PROGRAM] called [PROGRAM NAME]. Include: who it's for, what's different about it, the launch offer (if any), start date, and a clear CTA to sign up or DM for details. Energetic and direct.
💡 Pro tip: Launch offers (first-week free, founding member rate) create urgency that fills programs faster.
Prompt 13 of 15
Healthy recipe post (educational content)
Write a social media post sharing a [BREAKFAST/LUNCH/DINNER/SNACK] recipe that supports [FAT LOSS/MUSCLE BUILDING/PERFORMANCE]. Include: recipe name, macro breakdown (approximate), 5 ingredients, 3-step prep, and 1 pro tip for meal prep. Format for Instagram/Facebook. Include a CTA to follow for more recipes. 3 relevant hashtags.
💡 Pro tip: Nutrition content drives more saves (= algorithmic boost) than workout content on most platforms.
Prompt 14 of 15
Email nurture (lead from free download)
Write a 3-email nurture sequence for someone who downloaded my free [LEAD MAGNET — e.g., meal plan / home workout guide]. Email 1: Deliver the resource + introduce yourself. Email 2 (Day 3): One actionable tip they can use this week. Email 3 (Day 6): Introduce your coaching offer with a CTA. Each under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Free-to-paid conversion happens fastest when the nurture sequence proves your coaching value before pitching.
Prompt 15 of 15
Client birthday message
Write a brief, warm birthday message to send to a fitness client [NAME] on their birthday. Keep it personal but professional. Reference something specific about their fitness journey if possible. Under 75 words. Genuine, not a copy-paste corporate greeting.
💡 Pro tip: Birthday messages take 30 seconds and dramatically increase client loyalty and likelihood of referrals.

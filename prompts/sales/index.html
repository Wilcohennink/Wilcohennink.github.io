18 ChatGPT Prompts for Sales Professionals

Sales is about saying the right thing to the right person at the right time. These prompts help you say the right thing, faster — across cold outreach, follow-ups, objection handling, and closing.

Prompt 1 of 5
Cold email (personalized, non-spammy)
Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME] at [COMPANY]. Context: they recently [TRIGGER EVENT — e.g. raised funding, posted a job listing, published an article]. My offer: [OFFER IN ONE SENTENCE]. The email should: reference the trigger event specifically, lead with their problem (not my product), offer a concrete insight or value, and close with a low-friction CTA (15-minute call or a specific question). Under 100 words. Subject line included.
💡 Pro tip: Personalization with a trigger event (funding, hiring, news) gets 3x the response rate vs. generic cold email.
Prompt 2 of 5
LinkedIn connection request message
Write a LinkedIn connection request message to [PROSPECT] who is a [TITLE] at [COMPANY]. I'm reaching out because [REASON — common connection, their content, their company's initiative]. Keep it under 200 characters (LinkedIn limit), make it feel like a genuine human message, and do NOT pitch anything. Just get the connection.
💡 Pro tip: Never pitch in the connection request. That's a pattern interrupt — leads to instant ignore.
Prompt 3 of 5
Follow-up sequence (3 touches)
Write a 3-touch follow-up sequence for a prospect who didn't respond to my cold email about [OFFER]. Touch 1 (Day 3): Short, direct, reference the original email. Touch 2 (Day 7): Add new value — a relevant case study, stat, or insight. Touch 3 (Day 14): Break-up email with final ask. Each touch under 75 words. Do not be apologetic or needy.
💡 Pro tip: Most deals close on the 4th-8th touchpoint. Stop after 5 touches only if there's zero engagement.
Prompt 4 of 5
Objection handler (price objection)
Write a response to this objection: "[EXACT OBJECTION — e.g. 'Your price is too high']" for my product [PRODUCT] priced at [PRICE]. The response should: acknowledge the concern without being defensive, reframe around ROI/value, include a specific example or calculation, and re-ask for the business. Under 150 words. Tone: confident, not pushy.
💡 Pro tip: The worst response to a price objection is to immediately discount. Anchor value first.
Prompt 5 of 5
Proposal executive summary
Write an executive summary for a sales proposal for [PROSPECT COMPANY]. Their problem: [PROBLEM]. My solution: [SOLUTION]. Expected outcome: [SPECIFIC RESULT]. Investment: [PRICE]. The summary should be 150-200 words, lead with their pain not my credentials, include one social proof reference, and end with a clear next step. Tone: professional, confident.
💡 Pro tip: Decision makers often only read the executive summary. Make it self-contained and compelling.

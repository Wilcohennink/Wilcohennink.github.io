15 ChatGPT Prompts for E-Commerce Store Owners That Drive Sales

E-commerce is a conversion game. Every word on your site, in your emails, and in your ads either moves someone toward a purchase or away from it. These prompts are built to convert.

Prompt 1 of 15
Product description (benefit-led)
Write a product description for [PRODUCT NAME] priced at [PRICE]. Target customer: [AVATAR — e.g., busy mom, outdoor enthusiast]. Key features: [LIST 3-5 FEATURES]. Transform each feature into a customer benefit. Structure: 1-sentence hook, 3-4 sentences of benefits, a bulleted spec list (5 items), and a CTA. Under 150 words main copy. SEO keyword: [KEYWORD].
💡 Pro tip: Features tell, benefits sell. 'Waterproof' is a feature; 'hike in any weather without worrying' is a benefit.
Prompt 2 of 15
Abandoned cart email (sequence, email 1)
Write the first abandoned cart email for [STORE NAME]. The customer added [PRODUCT NAME] to their cart and didn't checkout. Email 1 (1 hour after abandonment): Short, helpful nudge — mention the product by name, include a link back to the cart, and ask if they had any questions. No discount yet. Under 100 words. Subject line included.
💡 Pro tip: The first abandoned cart email should not discount. Save the discount for email 3 — most people abandon for non-price reasons.
Prompt 3 of 15
Product launch email (new arrival)
Write a product launch email for [STORE NAME] announcing [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: a subject line that creates intrigue, 1-paragraph story about why this product was created, 3 key benefits, product photo placement note, price, and a CTA button. For email subscribers only — make them feel VIP. Launch offer: [OFFER if any]. Under 250 words.
💡 Pro tip: Launch emails sent to your subscriber list first (before the general public) create the 'insider' feeling that drives impulse buys.
Prompt 4 of 15
Facebook/Instagram ad copy (cold audience)
Write 3 Facebook/Instagram ad copy variations for [PRODUCT NAME] targeting [COLD AUDIENCE — describe demographics/interests]. Ad objective: [SALES/TRAFFIC/AWARENESS]. Format: Primary text (under 125 chars), Headline (under 27 chars), Description (under 27 chars). Version 1: problem-led, Version 2: social proof-led, Version 3: curiosity hook.
💡 Pro tip: Cold audience ads that lead with a relatable problem outperform product-feature ads by 3:1.
Prompt 5 of 15
Customer review request email
Write a post-purchase review request email for [STORE NAME], sent [X] days after delivery of [PRODUCT CATEGORY]. Thank the customer for their purchase, ask if everything arrived as expected, and invite them to leave a review with a direct link. Keep it short — the link is the CTA. Under 100 words. Friendly, not transactional.
💡 Pro tip: Review emails sent 7-14 days after delivery get 4x more responses than those sent at purchase.
Prompt 6 of 15
Collection page intro copy (SEO)
Write a short introduction paragraph (100-150 words) for the top of a [COLLECTION NAME] collection page on [STORE NAME]. Target keyword: '[KEYWORD]'. Include: what this collection is, who it's for, the key benefit, and a sentence mentioning 2-3 product types in the collection. Natural-sounding, SEO-friendly. Avoid keyword stuffing.
💡 Pro tip: Collection page copy with the target keyword in the first paragraph can move the page 3-5 positions in search results.
Prompt 7 of 15
Win-back email (lapsed customer)
Write a win-back email for [STORE NAME] targeting customers who haven't purchased in [X months]. Include: a subject line that gets reopened ('We miss you' alternatives), a brief note acknowledging the time gap, a compelling reason to come back ([NEW ARRIVALS/LOYALTY CREDITS/SPECIAL OFFER]), and a CTA. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Win-back email offers that include a time-limited incentive ('expires in 7 days') have 3x higher conversion rates.
Prompt 8 of 15
Influencer product seeding DM
Write a DM to send to [INFLUENCER TYPE — micro-influencer, niche creator] about sending them a free product from [STORE NAME]. Reference their content specifically ([POST or NICHE]). Explain what you're sending and why it's a fit for their audience. No hard ask — just offer to send it and let them share if they love it. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Micro-influencers (10k-100k followers) in a niche have 6x higher engagement than macro-influencers — and are far more accessible.
Prompt 9 of 15
Post-purchase upsell email
Write a post-purchase email for [STORE NAME] sent 2 days after [PRODUCT A] purchase. Suggest complementary product [PRODUCT B] with a brief explanation of why they go together. Include a limited-time discount ([X%]) for the upsell. CTA: Shop now. Under 150 words. Not pushy — helpful framing.
💡 Pro tip: Post-purchase emails have 5x higher open rates than standard marketing emails. Use that attention for your upsell.
Prompt 10 of 15
SMS campaign (flash sale)
Write a 3-part SMS campaign for a 24-hour flash sale at [STORE NAME]. SMS 1 (launch): Announce the sale, percentage off, link. Under 160 chars. SMS 2 (midday): Tease best sellers still in stock, urgency reminder. Under 160 chars. SMS 3 (2 hours before end): Final warning, countdown language. Under 160 chars.
💡 Pro tip: SMS flash sales sent to opted-in customers have 25-30% conversion rates — the highest of any marketing channel.
Prompt 11 of 15
FAQ page answers (reduce support tickets)
Write answers to these 5 common customer questions for [STORE NAME]: (1) How long does shipping take? (2) What's your return policy? (3) Are your products [sustainable/vegan/made in X]? (4) Do you ship internationally? (5) How do I track my order? Answers should be clear, specific to our policies ([POLICIES]), and under 75 words each.
💡 Pro tip: A good FAQ page reduces customer service volume by 25% — the same answers get asked thousands of times.
Prompt 12 of 15
Product bundle offer copy
Write copy for a [PRODUCT BUNDLE NAME] bundle at [STORE NAME] containing [PRODUCT 1], [PRODUCT 2], [PRODUCT 3]. Price: [BUNDLE PRICE] vs. [INDIVIDUAL PRICE]. Include: headline (savings-focused), brief description of each item, and why this combination makes sense for [TARGET CUSTOMER]. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Bundles increase average order value by 20-30% when the savings and use-together logic is made clear.
Prompt 13 of 15
Email subject line split test (5 options)
Write 5 email subject line variations for an email promoting [PRODUCT/SALE/OFFER] at [STORE NAME]. Include: 1 curiosity hook, 1 urgency/scarcity, 1 social proof/FOMO, 1 benefit-led, 1 personalization (using first name). Max 45 characters each. No spam trigger words.
💡 Pro tip: A/B test subject lines on every send. Over 50 emails, optimizing subject lines alone can double open rates.
Prompt 14 of 15
Homepage hero section copy
Write hero section copy for the homepage of [STORE NAME] selling [PRODUCT CATEGORY]. Include: headline (benefit-focused, under 8 words), sub-headline (who it's for + the key outcome, under 20 words), primary CTA button text (under 5 words), and a secondary trust line under the button ([GUARANTEE/REVIEW COUNT/BADGE]). 3 variations.
💡 Pro tip: Homepage hero headlines should answer one question: 'Why should I buy from you vs. anyone else?' in 8 words or less.
Prompt 15 of 15
Out-of-stock notification email (back-in-stock)
Write a back-in-stock notification email for customers who signed up to be notified about [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: subject line with the product name, 1-sentence excited opener, the product is back (with link), any details on limited quantity, and a strong CTA. Under 100 words. The product sells itself here — keep it short and clear.
💡 Pro tip: Back-in-stock emails have the highest click-to-purchase conversion rate of any transactional e-commerce email.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Event Planners That Win More Events and Wow More Clients

Event planning is relationship management at scale — vendors, venues, clients, and guests all need different communication. These prompts give you the templates for every scenario, from the first inquiry to the final thank-you.

Prompt 1 of 15
Event proposal (client-facing)
Write an event proposal for [CLIENT NAME]'s [EVENT TYPE] with approximately [GUEST COUNT] guests on [DATE] at [VENUE/LOCATION]. Include: event concept overview, proposed timeline, key vendors and services included, investment breakdown (broad line items), why our planning firm is the right choice (3 differentiators), and CTA to schedule a planning call. Under 500 words. Professional, creative.
💡 Pro tip: Event proposals that paint a picture of the finished event (not just a service list) win more business. Sell the vision, not the logistics.
Prompt 2 of 15
Venue inquiry email
Write an email to [VENUE NAME] inquiring about availability and pricing for a [EVENT TYPE] on [PREFERRED DATE(S)] for approximately [GUEST COUNT] guests. Include: event description (brief), setup requirements ([CEREMONY/DINNER/CORPORATE SETUP]), catering question (in-house or outside vendors allowed?), budget range (if comfortable sharing), and a request for a site visit. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Venue inquiry emails that include a budget range get faster, more relevant quotes — venues know whether to pitch their premium or standard package.
Prompt 3 of 15
Vendor brief (photographer/catering/band)
Write a vendor brief for [VENDOR TYPE — photographer/catering/entertainment] for [EVENT NAME] on [DATE]. Include: event overview (type, location, guest count), specific requirements for this vendor (5-6 bullet points), timeline they need to work within, how they'll coordinate with me, and budget range. Professional, organized. Under 250 words.
💡 Pro tip: Detailed vendor briefs produce accurate quotes on the first try and prevent surprises on the day.
Prompt 4 of 15
Client onboarding email (new booking)
Write an onboarding email to new client [NAME] who just booked [PLANNING PACKAGE] for their [EVENT TYPE] on [DATE]. Include: excitement and congratulations, what happens next (3 steps in the first 2 weeks), what they need to prepare or decide, and how to reach you. Under 200 words. Organized, warm, confidence-building.
💡 Pro tip: Clients who receive a clear onboarding plan make faster decisions and generate fewer anxious check-in calls.
Prompt 5 of 15
Day-of timeline template (event run sheet)
Create a detailed day-of timeline/run sheet for a [EVENT TYPE] with [GUEST COUNT] guests at [VENUE TYPE]. Include time slots from [START TIME] to [END TIME] with: setup windows, vendor arrival times, key event milestones ([CEREMONY/DINNER/SPEECHES/DANCING etc.]), buffer times for transitions, and responsible party for each item. Editable format.
💡 Pro tip: Day-of timelines with 10-15 minute buffers between key transitions prevent the cascading delays that define stressful events.
Prompt 6 of 15
Social media post (event highlight)
Write 3 Instagram caption variations for a highlight post from a recent [EVENT TYPE] at [VENUE/CITY]. Version 1: venue/atmosphere focused. Version 2: client story (with permission). Version 3: behind-the-scenes planning moment. 3-5 relevant hashtags each. Under 200 words per caption. Evocative and emotional — events are about feelings.
💡 Pro tip: Event highlight posts with an emotional angle ('this is why we do what we do') outperform vendor-tag posts in reach and saves.
Prompt 7 of 15
Email to non-converting inquiry (follow-up)
Write a follow-up email to an event inquiry from [CLIENT NAME] who hasn't responded in [X] days. Re-express your enthusiasm for their event, ask if they have any questions about the proposal, and offer to adjust the scope or schedule a brief call. Not pushy. Under 100 words. One last genuine outreach.
💡 Pro tip: 50% of event bookings happen after 2+ follow-up touches. One follow-up email after silence is almost always worth sending.
Prompt 8 of 15
Post-event wrap-up email (to client)
Write a post-event wrap-up email to client [NAME] after their [EVENT TYPE] on [DATE]. Include: genuine reflection on how the day went, what you're proud of about the event, any final administrative notes (vendor invoices, final photo/video timelines), and an invitation to leave a review or refer a friend. Under 200 words. Warm, closing a successful chapter.
💡 Pro tip: Post-event emails that reflect on a specific memorable moment feel personal and generate the most genuine Google/social reviews.
Prompt 9 of 15
Referral request (to past client)
Write an email to past client [NAME] whose [EVENT TYPE] you planned in [YEAR]. Express continued appreciation for the experience, reference a specific detail you loved about their event, and let them know you'd welcome a referral if they know anyone planning a [WEDDING/CORPORATE EVENT/BIRTHDAY] in the next year. Under 120 words. Genuine, relationship-first.
💡 Pro tip: Event planning referrals from past clients convert at 60%+ — no other marketing comes close. Every past client relationship is worth maintaining.
Prompt 10 of 15
Styled shoot invitation (to vendors)
Write an invitation to [VENDOR TYPE — photographer, florist, caterer] to participate in a styled shoot for [EVENT TYPE] planned by [YOUR COMPANY NAME] on [DATE] at [LOCATION]. Cover: the concept and aesthetic, what vendors will receive (images for portfolio, social features, credits), what they need to contribute, and logistics. Under 250 words. Collaborative, exciting.
💡 Pro tip: Styled shoots create portfolio content that attracts ideal clients — the ROI is measured in the quality of future inquiries, not cash.
Prompt 11 of 15
Preferred vendor list pitch (to venue)
Write an email to [VENUE NAME]'s events coordinator requesting inclusion on their preferred vendor list as an event planner. Include: brief description of your services and planning style, 2-3 types of events you specialize in, how being on their preferred list benefits their clients, any events you've already hosted there (if applicable). Under 200 words. Professional, mutually beneficial framing.
💡 Pro tip: Venue preferred vendor relationships are the most sustainable lead source for event planners — invest in them like partnerships.
Prompt 12 of 15
Wedding ceremony script (civil non-religious)
Write a simple, modern civil wedding ceremony script for [COUPLE NAMES] on [DATE]. Include: officiant welcome (1-2 minutes), brief acknowledgment of guests, declaration of intent (2 exchanges), vows (2 short, adaptable vow options), ring exchange, pronouncement, and first kiss/presentation. Tone: warm, slightly poetic, inclusive. Under 500 words total. Customizable brackets [LIKE THIS] for personal details.
💡 Pro tip: Having a ceremony script draft ready for couples who don't know where to start is an unexpected differentiator — most planners don't offer this.
Prompt 13 of 15
Sponsorship package for industry event
Write a sponsorship package for [EVENT NAME], a [TYPE OF INDUSTRY/COMMUNITY EVENT] organized by [COMPANY/ORGANIZATION] on [DATE]. Include 3 tiers: Gold ($[X]), Silver ($[Y]), Bronze ($[Z]). For each tier: list of benefits (brand exposure, speaking time, attendee list access, etc.), and brief positioning statement. Add an 'a la carte' options section. Under 400 words.
💡 Pro tip: Event sponsorship packages with 3 tiers attract more total sponsorship dollars than single-tier offerings — give sponsors options to fit their budget.
Prompt 14 of 15
Guest communication email (event day logistics)
Write a guest communication email for attendees of [EVENT NAME] on [DATE] at [VENUE]. Include: arrival time guidance, parking/transport instructions, dress code (if applicable), what to bring (ticket/registration confirmation), schedule highlights, and contact for day-of questions. Under 200 words. Clear, organized, reduces guest anxiety.
💡 Pro tip: Day-of logistics emails sent 48 hours before the event reduce 'where should I park?' questions by 70%.
Prompt 15 of 15
Google Business / directory profile description
Write a business description for [EVENT PLANNING COMPANY] for Google Business, Yelp, or The Knot. Cover: types of events planned, service area, planning style/specialties, what clients say about working with you (one testimonial paraphrase), and how to inquire. Under 200 words. Keyword-rich: include '[EVENT TYPE] planner [CITY]' naturally.
💡 Pro tip: Event planning directory profiles with a clear specialty ('we specialize in intimate weddings under 100 guests') attract better-fit inquiries than generalist descriptions.

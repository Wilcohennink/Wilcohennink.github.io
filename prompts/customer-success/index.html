15 ChatGPT Prompts for Customer Success That Drive Retention and Expansion

Customer success is about making your customers successful — and communicating that success back to them and your organization. These prompts cover every touchpoint from onboarding to renewal, helping you scale your impact without burning out.

Prompt 1 of 15
Onboarding welcome email (new customer)
Write an onboarding welcome email to new customer [COMPANY NAME], specifically to champion [CHAMPION NAME]. Include: excitement about the partnership, overview of what happens in the first 30 days (3 milestones), their dedicated CSM (you) and contact info, the first action item with a deadline, and a link to any onboarding resources. Under 250 words. Organized and warm.
💡 Pro tip: Customers who have a clear picture of the first 30 days are 50% less likely to go dark in early onboarding.
Prompt 2 of 15
QBR / EBR deck narrative
Write the narrative sections for a Quarterly Business Review (QBR) deck for customer [COMPANY NAME]. Sections: (1) Partnership recap (shared goals, agreed milestones), (2) Value delivered this quarter (metrics: [INSERT METRICS]), (3) Challenges and how we addressed them, (4) Roadmap for next quarter (3 priorities), (5) Success metrics we're tracking, (6) Open items and asks. Each section: 3-5 bullet points. Under 400 words total.
💡 Pro tip: QBRs that lead with value delivered (before asking for renewals or expansions) have 30% higher renewal rates.
Prompt 3 of 15
At-risk customer intervention email
Write an email to at-risk customer [COMPANY NAME] (champion: [NAME]) whose usage has declined by [X%] over [TIMEFRAME]. Acknowledge the drop without making it awkward. Ask an open-ended question about what's changed. Offer a specific resource or check-in call. Do NOT mention renewal or pricing. Under 150 words. Genuine concern, not alarm.
💡 Pro tip: At-risk outreach that leads with curiosity ('what's changed?') rather than urgency gets 3x more honest replies.
Prompt 4 of 15
Renewal conversation email (60-90 days out)
Write a renewal conversation opener email to customer [COMPANY NAME] 60-90 days before their contract renewal date. Lead with their achievements and value metrics ([METRICS]). Frame renewal as a natural continuation of a successful partnership. Invite a call to review the next term and explore any new needs. No pressure language. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Renewal conversations started 90 days out have 20% higher NRR than those started 30 days out. Early beats late every time.
Prompt 5 of 15
Expansion / upsell email (to champion)
Write an email to champion [NAME] at [COMPANY] introducing an expansion opportunity: [PRODUCT/FEATURE UPGRADE or ADD-ON]. Lead with how their current usage patterns suggest this would add value. Include: what the expansion includes, estimated impact on their [SPECIFIC GOAL/METRIC], and a CTA to discuss. Under 200 words. Consultative, not sales-y.
💡 Pro tip: Expansion emails that connect the offer to the customer's specific usage data convert at 4x the rate of generic upsell pitches.
Prompt 6 of 15
Churn notification response email (customer cancelling)
Write a response email to customer [COMPANY NAME] who has notified you they want to cancel. Acknowledge their decision without pushback, express genuine appreciation for the partnership, ask one question to understand the reason (learning, not retention attempt), and offer a brief exit conversation if they're open to it. Under 150 words. Gracious, professional.
💡 Pro tip: Customers who receive gracious exit responses are 30% more likely to return or refer others. Never let a customer leave angry.
Prompt 7 of 15
Health score summary (internal handoff)
Write an internal customer health summary for [CUSTOMER NAME] to hand off to a new CSM or for QBR prep. Cover: overall health rating ([RED/YELLOW/GREEN]), usage data ([KEY METRICS]), relationship status (sponsor access, champion engagement), open issues, renewal timeline, and top 3 risks and opportunities. Under 250 words. Factual, no spin.
💡 Pro tip: Clean internal handoff notes prevent 'I didn't know they were at-risk' surprises that cause churn during CSM transitions.
Prompt 8 of 15
Executive sponsor outreach (new relationship)
Write an introductory email from me (CSM) to the executive sponsor at customer [COMPANY NAME]: [EXEC NAME, TITLE]. Briefly introduce myself, reference the partnership goal, share one meaningful win since onboarding ([RESULT]), and request a 30-minute alignment call to understand their strategic priorities. Under 150 words. Executive-level tone.
💡 Pro tip: Customers with active executive sponsor relationships churn at half the rate of those where the only relationship is with the champion.
Prompt 9 of 15
Customer success metrics report (internal)
Write a monthly customer success team metrics report for [MONTH]. Include: NRR ([AMOUNT/%]), GRR ([AMOUNT/%]), new churned ARR ([AMOUNT]), expansion ARR ([AMOUNT]), average health score ([SCORE]), customers at risk ([NUMBER]), QBRs completed ([NUMBER]). Add a 3-bullet narrative: what drove this month's results, what to watch, and next month's priorities. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: CS metrics reports that include forward-looking risks (not just historical numbers) help leadership allocate headcount before churn hits.
Prompt 10 of 15
Product feedback loop email (to product team)
Write an internal email to the product team summarizing customer feedback on [FEATURE/AREA] gathered from [X] CSM conversations over [TIMEFRAME]. Include: top 3 pain points (with customer quotes), 2 feature requests that came up repeatedly, 1 positive pattern (what's working), and a recommended priority order for the product team to consider. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: CS → product feedback emails with direct customer quotes are actioned faster than those with only summary statistics.
Prompt 11 of 15
Onboarding milestone email (mid-onboarding check-in)
Write a mid-onboarding check-in email to [CUSTOMER CHAMPION] at [COMPANY] who is [X] weeks into their onboarding. Celebrate the progress made ([MILESTONE REACHED]), confirm the next milestone, surface any blockers or support needs, and express confidence in their trajectory. Under 150 words. Progress-affirming.
💡 Pro tip: Mid-onboarding milestones reduce the 'Is this going to work?' anxiety that causes early churn.
Prompt 12 of 15
Case study request (to happy customer)
Write an email to champion [NAME] at [COMPANY] requesting a case study feature. Explain: the format (brief interview, you write the story), the potential exposure ([WHERE IT WILL BE FEATURED]), and what it involves (30-minute call + review). Emphasize it's a collaboration, not a testimonial. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Framing case studies as collaborative stories (not testimonials you'll write for them) gets 3x more yes responses.
Prompt 13 of 15
Training session follow-up email
Write a follow-up email to customer team attendees after a [PRODUCT/FEATURE] training session on [DATE]. Include: key topics covered (3 bullets), links to recording and resources, 3 quick-start actions they can take this week, and an invitation to ask follow-up questions. Under 200 words. Reinforcement-focused.
💡 Pro tip: Training follow-up emails sent within 24 hours double the rate of skill adoption compared to those sent later.
Prompt 14 of 15
NPS follow-up email (detractor response)
Write a response email to a customer [NAME] at [COMPANY] who gave an NPS score of [SCORE] with the comment: '[COMMENT]'. Acknowledge their feedback specifically, express genuine appreciation for their honesty, ask one clarifying question (to understand the root cause), and commit to a follow-up conversation. Under 150 words. Empathetic, accountable.
💡 Pro tip: Responding to every detractor score (not just promoters) within 48 hours is the highest-leverage NPS action you can take.
Prompt 15 of 15
Escalation email (to internal team)
Write an internal escalation email to [MANAGER/VP/PRODUCT TEAM] about an at-risk situation with customer [COMPANY NAME]. Cover: current situation, risk level (RED — churn likely within [TIMEFRAME]), what's driving it ([ROOT CAUSE]), what's been tried, what you need from leadership ([SPECIFIC ASK]), and recommended next steps. Under 250 words. Factual, action-oriented.
💡 Pro tip: Escalation emails with a specific ask ('I need an executive call from our VP this week') get resolved 5x faster than those that just describe the problem.

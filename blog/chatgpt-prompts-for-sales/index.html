50 ChatGPT Prompts for Sales That Actually Close Deals (2026)

Sales is about speed, personalization, and persistence. Most salespeople are slow, generic, and give up too early. ChatGPT changes the speed equation — but only if you have the right prompts.

These 50 ChatGPT prompts for sales are organized by stage of the funnel. Copy them, customize the brackets, and use them today.

How to Use These Prompts

Every prompt uses bracketed variables like [job title] or [product name]. Fill those in before you paste. The more specific you are, the better the output.

Pro tip: Add this context at the top of a new chat: "You are a sales copywriter for [your company]. We sell [product] to [audience]. Our tone is [professional/casual/direct]. Keep all output concise and human."

Cold Outreach Prompts (1–10)

1. Cold email first touch

"Write a cold email to a [job title] at a [industry] company with [X] employees. We help companies like theirs [outcome]. One proof point: [result]. CTA: 10-minute call. Under 100 words. No corporate speak."

2. LinkedIn connection request

"Write a LinkedIn connection request to [role] at [company type]. Keep it under 280 characters. Reference [shared interest/context]. Don't pitch in the request."

3. LinkedIn DM after connecting

"Write a brief LinkedIn message to [name] after they accepted my connection request. Soft intro to what I do, no hard pitch. Under 100 words."

4. Referral outreach email

"Write a cold email to [prospect name] at [company]. I was referred by [mutual contact]. We help [job title]s [outcome]. Keep it conversational and under 80 words."

5. Event follow-up email

"Write a follow-up email to [name] after meeting at [event]. Reference one specific thing we discussed. Suggest a next step: [call/demo/coffee]. Keep it warm and brief."

6. Video prospecting script

"Write a 30-second video prospecting script for [job title] at [company]. Open with something specific to their business. Explain what we do in one sentence. End with a soft CTA."

7. Cold email subject line pack

"Write 10 subject line variations for a cold email to [job title] in [industry]. Use different angles: curiosity, specificity, social proof, question, direct. Keep each under 50 characters."

8. Re-engagement email for ghosted prospects

"Write an email to [name] who went dark after [last interaction]. Don't guilt-trip them. Add a new piece of value: [new case study/stat/idea]. Close with a breakup email option."

9. Outbound to recently funded company

"Write a cold email congratulating [company] on their recent [Series A/B] funding. Tie their funding goals to how we can help them [outcome]. Keep it timely and relevant."

10. Seasonal/timely outreach angle

"Write a cold email to [prospect] tying our offer to [current trend/challenge in their industry]. Make it feel like now is the right time to talk. Under 90 words."

Follow-Up Prompts (11–20)

11. Follow-up after no response

"Write a follow-up email to [name] who didn't reply to my first email about [topic]. Add one new piece of value. Keep it under 60 words. Don't apologize for following up."

12. Second follow-up (3rd touch)

"Write a third-touch follow-up to [name] about [offer]. This should be brief, a little more direct, and include a soft breakup line if they're not interested. Under 50 words."

13. Follow-up after a demo

"Write a post-demo follow-up email to [name] at [company]. Recap the 3 key things we discussed: [point 1], [point 2], [point 3]. Include next steps and a deadline if applicable."

14. Follow-up after sending a proposal

"Write a follow-up email to [name] who received our proposal [X] days ago. Reference the proposal without reattaching it. Ask if they have questions. Low pressure CTA."

15. Follow-up to get a decision

"Write a polite but direct follow-up to [name] who has been in 'thinking about it' mode for [X weeks]. Create urgency without being aggressive. Offer to address any remaining concerns."

16. Multi-stakeholder follow-up

"Write an email to [name] who said they need to run it by [decision maker]. Offer to include the decision maker in a brief call or provide materials they can share internally."

17. Follow-up with social proof

"Write a follow-up email to a warm prospect who expressed interest but went quiet. Include one new customer success story: [client type] achieved [result] in [timeframe]."

18. Voicemail script

"Write a 20-second voicemail script for [prospect]. I'm following up on [context]. CTA is to call back or reply to my email. Keep it casual, not scripted-sounding."

19. SMS follow-up

"Write a professional but brief SMS message to [name] following up on our [demo/call/proposal]. Under 160 characters. CTA: reply yes/no."

20. Breakup email (final touch)

"Write a final 'breakup' email to [name] I've been trying to reach for [X weeks]. Close the loop gracefully, leave the door open for the future, and keep it under 5 sentences."

Discovery & Qualification Prompts (21–28)

21. Discovery call question list

"Generate 10 open-ended discovery questions for a call with [job title] at a [company type]. Focus on understanding their current process, pain points, budget indicators, and decision-making timeline."

22. Budget qualification questions

"Write 5 non-awkward ways to ask about budget during a sales conversation with [prospect type]. Make them feel natural, not pushy."

23. Pre-call research prompt

"I have a sales call with [name] from [company] tomorrow. Based on their [LinkedIn/website/press], generate a quick brief: likely priorities, recent news, good icebreakers, and angles for positioning our [product]."

24. MEDDIC qualification checklist

"Using the MEDDIC framework, write qualification questions for a [product type] sale to [company type]. Cover Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, and Champion."

25. Competitor differentiation prep

"I'm selling [product] to a prospect who currently uses [competitor]. Write 5 questions to understand how they're using it, what frustrates them, and where they see gaps — without bashing the competitor."

26. Multi-stakeholder mapping prompt

"Help me map the stakeholders for a deal at a [company type] buying [product type]. What roles are typically involved, what does each care about, and how should I message to each?"

27. Pain point clarification

"Write 5 probing follow-up questions to go deeper after a prospect says '[initial pain statement]'. Help me understand the root cause, business impact, and why it hasn't been solved yet."

28. Urgency creation questions

"Write 5 questions I can ask during a discovery call to naturally surface urgency — without manufacturing false urgency. Help the prospect articulate why solving this now matters."

Objection Handling Prompts (29–36)

29. "It's too expensive"

"Write 3 responses to a prospect who says our price is too high. Frame the cost in terms of ROI. Don't discount immediately. One response should explore what 'expensive' means to them."

30. "We already have a solution"

"Write a response to 'we already use [competitor] for this.' Acknowledge their current solution, ask one question to uncover gaps, and position ours as complementary or superior in one specific way."

31. "We need to think about it"

"Write 3 ways to respond to 'we need to think about it' without being pushy. Help uncover what's really behind the hesitation while moving the conversation forward."

32. "Send me more information"

"Write a response to 'just send me more info' — a classic stall. Acknowledge the request but also ask one qualifying question to understand what information would actually be useful."

33. "Not the right time"

"Write a response to 'the timing isn't right.' Acknowledge it, ask what would make timing better, and plant a seed for follow-up in [X months]."

34. "I need to run it by my team"

"Write a response to 'I need to get my team's buy-in.' Offer to facilitate that process — a brief call with the team, a one-page summary they can share, or materials I can provide."

35. "We don't have the budget"

"Write 3 responses to 'we don't have budget right now.' One should explore if there's a smaller entry point. One should address ROI. One should set up a future conversation."

36. "We tried something similar and it didn't work"

"Write a response to 'we tried something like this before and it failed.' Acknowledge the past experience, ask what went wrong, and position how our approach is different."

Proposals & Closing Prompts (37–44)

37. Proposal executive summary

"Write a 200-word executive summary for a proposal to [company type]. We're offering [service/product] at [price]. Their main goals are [goal 1] and [goal 2]. Make it outcome-focused."

38. ROI calculator narrative

"Write a 3-paragraph ROI narrative for [prospect] who spends [X hours/dollars] on [current problem]. Our solution reduces that by [Y%]. Frame it in terms of annual value, not just savings."

39. Proposal follow-up sequence

"Write a 3-email sequence to follow up on a proposal sent to [name]. Email 1: sent same day, friendly recap. Email 2: 3 days later, address likely objections. Email 3: 7 days later, create urgency."

40. Verbal close script

"Write a verbal close script for a [product] sale to [prospect type]. After addressing their questions, how do I ask for the business in a way that feels natural and confident?"

41. Contract kickoff email

"Write an email to send after a deal is signed. Congratulate them, confirm next steps, introduce the team, and set expectations for the first week. Warm and professional."

42. Upsell pitch

"Write a message to an existing customer about upgrading to [higher tier/add-on]. They've been on [current plan] for [X months]. Reference their success and how the upgrade would accelerate it."

43. Case study pitch in proposal

"Write a 150-word case study paragraph for a proposal about [client type] who achieved [result] in [timeframe] using [product]. Include the challenge, solution, and outcome."

44. Pricing page copy

"Write copy for a 3-tier pricing table for [product]. Tiers: [names]. Highlight the recommended tier. For each, list 4–5 key features and a one-line description of who it's for."

Retention & Expansion Prompts (45–50)

45. Quarterly business review agenda

"Create a QBR agenda for a customer who has been using [product] for [X months]. Include sections on: usage data review, ROI achieved, challenges, expansion opportunities, and goals for next quarter."

46. Customer check-in email

"Write a check-in email to a customer who hasn't engaged with us in [X weeks]. Keep it helpful, not salesy. Offer one specific resource or tip relevant to their use case."

47. Renewal preparation email

"Write an email to a customer whose contract renews in 30 days. Highlight their results, address any known concerns, and make the renewal process feel easy and obvious."

48. Expansion opportunity email

"Write an email to [customer name] introducing a new product/feature that complements what they're already using. Lead with how it solves [specific challenge they have], not product features."

49. NPS follow-up for promoters

"Write a message to a customer who gave us a 9 or 10 NPS score. Thank them, ask for a referral (if appropriate), and invite them to share a testimonial."

50. Win-back email for churned customers

"Write a re-engagement email to a customer who cancelled [X months] ago. Acknowledge why they left (if known), highlight what's changed since then, and offer a low-barrier way to try again."

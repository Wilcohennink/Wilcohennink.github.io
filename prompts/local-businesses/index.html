15 ChatGPT Prompts for Local Businesses That Drive Foot Traffic and Calls

Local businesses win by being visible, trusted, and top-of-mind in their community. These prompts help you create the Google posts, social content, review responses, and promotions that keep your business front and center.

Prompt 1 of 15
Google Business Profile post (weekly update)
Write a Google Business Profile post for [BUSINESS NAME] in [CITY]. Topic: [WEEKLY SPECIAL/PROMOTION/SERVICE HIGHLIGHT]. Include: 1-2 sentences of engaging copy, the key offer or info, a CTA (call, visit, or book online), and your business hours if relevant. Under 150 words. Keyword-rich but natural: include '[CITY] [BUSINESS TYPE]' naturally.
💡 Pro tip: Businesses that post on Google Business weekly appear in local search results 40% more often than those that post monthly.
Prompt 2 of 15
Review response (positive Google/Yelp)
Write a response to this positive customer review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Thank them by name, reference something specific they mentioned, add one piece of info they (and future readers) might find valuable, and invite them back. Under 100 words. Personal, not templated.
💡 Pro tip: Every review response is public marketing. The person reading it is deciding whether to visit — write for them too.
Prompt 3 of 15
Review response (negative Google/Yelp)
Write a professional response to this negative review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Acknowledge the experience without being defensive, apologize sincerely, invite them to contact you directly to resolve it ([EMAIL/PHONE]). Do not argue or make excuses. Under 100 words. De-escalate and demonstrate accountability.
💡 Pro tip: Potential customers read negative reviews to see how you respond. A gracious response converts more readers than a perfect 5-star rating.
Prompt 4 of 15
Email promotion (local event or sale)
Write an email promotion for [BUSINESS NAME] announcing [PROMOTION/EVENT — e.g., summer sale, anniversary special, new service]. Include: subject line, hook sentence, promotion details (what/when/how to redeem), a CTA, and expiry date for urgency. Under 200 words. Warm, local-community tone.
💡 Pro tip: Local promotions with a clear expiry date convert 3x better than open-ended offers.
Prompt 5 of 15
Facebook/Instagram post (community-building)
Write a Facebook or Instagram post for [BUSINESS NAME] in [CITY] that builds community connection rather than promoting a product. Topic options: [BEHIND-THE-SCENES / LOCAL SHOUTOUT / TEAM MEMBER SPOTLIGHT / COMMUNITY INVOLVEMENT]. Under 150 words. Authentic, story-driven. 3-5 relevant hashtags.
💡 Pro tip: Community-building posts get 4x more organic reach than promotional posts on Facebook and Instagram. They also drive more word-of-mouth.
Prompt 6 of 15
Referral program launch (customer email)
Write an email to existing customers announcing a referral program for [BUSINESS NAME]. Cover: how it works (refer a friend → both get [REWARD]), how easy it is to participate (1-2 steps), and why their referral matters to the business. Under 150 words. Make them feel like VIPs who are being trusted with this.
💡 Pro tip: Local business referral programs work best when the reward goes to both referrer AND referee — it removes the 'guilt' of recommending.
Prompt 7 of 15
SMS campaign (appointment reminder / flash offer)
Write 2 SMS messages for [BUSINESS NAME]: (1) An appointment reminder sent 24 hours before: include name, time, address, and cancellation option. Under 160 characters. (2) A flash offer to opted-in customers: the deal, expiry (today/48 hours), and how to redeem. Under 160 characters. Both must comply with SMS marketing rules (opt-out option).
💡 Pro tip: Appointment reminder SMS messages reduce no-show rates by 30-50%. The ROI pays for the entire SMS tool in week 1.
Prompt 8 of 15
New service/product announcement post
Write a social media post for [BUSINESS NAME] announcing a new [SERVICE/PRODUCT]: [NAME]. Include: what it is (1-2 sentences), who it's for, what problem it solves, price or starting price (if public), how to book/buy/inquire, and launch date or availability. Platform: [Facebook/Instagram/Nextdoor]. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: New service announcement posts with a specific launch date create a reason to act now — which drives immediate responses.
Prompt 9 of 15
Yelp / directory profile description
Write a business description for [BUSINESS NAME] on Yelp or a local directory. Cover: what you do, who you serve, your location and service area, what makes you different from competitors (2-3 differentiators), and your best reviewed service or product. Under 250 words. Keyword-rich: include '[CITY] [BUSINESS TYPE]' naturally. No promotional fluff.
💡 Pro tip: Directory descriptions with specific differentiators ('we've been family-owned for 20 years') convert browsers to callers at 2x the rate of generic descriptions.
Prompt 10 of 15
Holiday promotion campaign (3-piece)
Write a 3-piece holiday promotion campaign for [BUSINESS NAME] around [HOLIDAY]. Piece 1: Teaser social post (build anticipation, don't reveal the offer). Piece 2: Launch email (reveal the promotion, details, and CTA). Piece 3: Last-chance social post (urgency, ends [DATE]). Tie everything to your brand personality. Holiday: [HOLIDAY/SEASON].
💡 Pro tip: 3-step campaigns (tease, launch, last chance) generate 40% more total responses than a single promotion post.
Prompt 11 of 15
Nextdoor announcement (local neighborhood)
Write a Nextdoor post for [BUSINESS NAME] announcing [NEWS/PROMOTION/SERVICE]. Nextdoor tone: neighbor-to-neighbor, local, conversational. Include: what the business does (briefly — assume some neighbors don't know you), the announcement, a special offer for Nextdoor neighbors ([OFFER]), and contact info. Under 200 words. Community-first, not sales-y.
💡 Pro tip: Nextdoor posts that reference the neighborhood ('we've served [NEIGHBORHOOD] families for X years') perform far better than generic business announcements.
Prompt 12 of 15
Email newsletter (monthly local update)
Write a monthly email newsletter for [BUSINESS NAME]. Sections: (1) Brief update or story from the business (2-3 sentences), (2) Featured service/product with a special offer, (3) A local community note or tip relevant to your audience, (4) Reminder of hours/contact/booking link. Under 300 words. Warm, local, relationship-building.
💡 Pro tip: Monthly newsletters that mix business updates with local community content are read at 2x the rate of purely promotional emails.
Prompt 13 of 15
Thank-you note (after service)
Write a brief thank-you note to send to a customer [NAME] after their [SERVICE/PURCHASE] at [BUSINESS NAME]. Express genuine appreciation, reference something specific about their visit or purchase if possible, and invite them back (mention any upcoming promotion or seasonal service). Under 75 words. Personal, warm.
💡 Pro tip: Physical or SMS thank-you notes sent within 24 hours of a purchase increase repeat visit likelihood by 25%.
Prompt 14 of 15
Team member spotlight post
Write a social media spotlight post featuring team member [NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME]. Include: their role, how long they've been with the business, one thing they love about their work, a fun personal detail, and a line about how to request/find them. Under 150 words. Make them feel celebrated.
💡 Pro tip: Team spotlights humanize your business and consistently outperform product posts in engagement — customers trust faces more than logos.
Prompt 15 of 15
Welcome email (new subscriber / loyalty signup)
Write a welcome email for someone who just joined [BUSINESS NAME]'s email list or loyalty program. Include: a warm welcome, what they can expect (frequency, content, exclusive offers), an immediate reward for signing up ([DISCOUNT/FREE ITEM/OFFER]), and how to redeem it. Under 150 words. Make them feel glad they signed up.
💡 Pro tip: Welcome emails are the most-read emails you'll ever send — 60%+ open rate. Include your best offer here.

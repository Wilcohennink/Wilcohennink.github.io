20 ChatGPT Prompts for Social Media That Get Engagement

Social media writing is 80% formula, 20% originality. These prompts give you the formula — you add the originality. Expect to cut your content creation time by 70%.

Prompt 1 of 5
LinkedIn hook post (authority-building)
Write a LinkedIn post about "[TOPIC/LESSON]" in my field ([YOUR FIELD]). Format: Start with a single punchy sentence that stops the scroll. Follow with 3-5 short paragraphs sharing a specific insight or story. Add a 3-item takeaway list. End with a question to drive comments. Max 1,200 characters. Tone: direct, confident, no corporate fluff.
💡 Pro tip: The first line determines whether LinkedIn shows 'See more'. Make it count.
Prompt 2 of 5
Twitter/X thread (viral format)
Write a 7-tweet thread about "[TOPIC]". Format: Tweet 1 = bold hook with a promise. Tweets 2-6 = one concrete point each, with an example or stat. Tweet 7 = summary + CTA to follow for more. Each tweet must be under 280 characters. No filler words. The thread should make someone screenshot Tweet 1.
💡 Pro tip: Threads with 'Tweet 1 = bold claim' format get 3x more impressions than informational starts.
Prompt 3 of 5
Instagram caption (storytelling format)
Write an Instagram caption for a post about "[TOPIC/IMAGE DESCRIPTION]". Structure: 1-2 sentence hook, 3-4 lines of story or value, 1 line CTA. Include 5 relevant hashtags at the end. Tone: [CASUAL/PROFESSIONAL/INSPIRATIONAL]. Max 300 characters before the fold.
💡 Pro tip: Keep the hook above the 'more' fold — Instagram shows only 125 characters before cutting off.
Prompt 4 of 5
Content calendar (7 days, one theme)
Create a 7-day social media content calendar for "[BRAND/ACCOUNT]" around the theme "[THEME]". For each day include: platform (choose from LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram), content type (educational, behind-the-scenes, promotional, engagement), post idea summary (1 sentence), and one hook line. Target audience: [AUDIENCE].
💡 Pro tip: Use this to batch-create content on Mondays. Reduce decision fatigue by planning themes monthly.
Prompt 5 of 5
Comment reply generator (thought leadership)
Write 5 thoughtful comment replies for a post about "[TOPIC]". The comments should: add a new perspective or data point, agree or respectfully disagree with a reason, invite further discussion, and be 2-4 sentences each. My field: [YOUR FIELD]. My viewpoint: [YOUR STANCE/ANGLE].
💡 Pro tip: Commenting strategically on high-engagement posts in your niche drives more profile views than most posts.

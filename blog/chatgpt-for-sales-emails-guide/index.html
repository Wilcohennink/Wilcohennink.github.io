How to Use ChatGPT for Sales Emails That Actually Get Replies

Most ChatGPT cold emails are immediately obvious — and immediately deleted.

Generic opener. Vague value prop. Weak CTA. The recipient can tell it was written by AI in five seconds.

But ChatGPT can write sales emails that get replies — when you use it correctly. Here's how.

Why Most AI Sales Emails Fail

The failure isn't ChatGPT. The failure is the prompt.

Bad prompt:

"Write a cold email to sell my marketing service."

What you get: A generic, stiff email that sounds like every other cold email in the prospect's inbox.

Good prompt:

"Write a cold email to a Head of Marketing at a 50-person B2B SaaS company. Our service: we build conversion-optimised landing pages. Their likely pain: slow launch timelines and pages that don't convert. Keep it under 100 words. Open with a specific insight about their industry, not a compliment. End with a low-friction CTA — a 15-minute call, not 'I'd love to connect.'"

What you get: Something specific, relevant, and worth reading.

The difference is context and constraints.

The 5-Part Prompt Formula for Sales Emails

Every good sales email prompt has these five components:

1. Recipient profile

Who are you writing to? Job title, company type, company size. The more specific, the better.

2. Your offer

What do you do? What's the specific benefit? Not "we help businesses grow" — what do you actually produce for them?

3. Their pain point

What problem are they most likely struggling with right now? This should be based on research, not guesswork.

4. Constraints

Word count. Tone. Format. What to avoid (buzzwords, filler, corporate speak).

5. The CTA

One specific next step. Make it low-friction.

10 ChatGPT Prompts for Sales Emails (Copy & Paste)

Prompt 1: The Cold Outreach Email

"Write a cold outreach email to a [job title] at a [company type] company with [X] employees. I sell [what you sell]. Their biggest challenge is likely [pain point]. Keep it under 90 words. Don't start with 'I hope this email finds you well.' Open with an observation or insight. End with a CTA to book a 15-minute call this week."

Prompt 2: The Follow-Up (No Reply)

"Write a follow-up email to [name] who hasn't replied to my email about [topic]. I sent it [X] days ago. Don't reference or apologise for the previous email. Just add one new piece of value — a stat, insight, or question — and end with a softer CTA. Under 60 words."

Prompt 3: The Re-Engagement Email (Cold Leads)

"Write a re-engagement email to someone who expressed interest in [product/service] 3 months ago but went cold. Don't mention the gap. Lead with something new — a feature update, result we got for a client, or a relevant industry trend. Offer something to restart the conversation. Under 100 words."

Prompt 4: Post-Meeting Follow-Up

"Write a follow-up email after a discovery call with [prospect]. Main things they shared: [2-3 pain points]. What we proposed: [brief summary]. Next step discussed: [e.g., sending a proposal]. Keep it warm, recap 3 bullet points from the call, and confirm the next step with a specific date."

Prompt 5: The Inbound Lead Response

"Write a response to a lead who just filled out our contact form. They said: [paste what they wrote]. Our service: [what you offer]. They want a quick response that confirms you received it, shows you understood what they're looking for, and books a call. Keep it under 100 words. Warm and direct."

Prompt 6: The Referral Request

"Write an email asking a happy client for referrals. They've been working with us for [X months]. We helped them achieve [result]. Keep it conversational and specific — not a form letter. Give them an easy way to refer (forward this email, LinkedIn intro, etc.). Under 120 words."

Prompt 7: The Multi-Touch Sequence (3 Emails)

"Write a 3-email outreach sequence targeting [audience]. Each email spaced 4 days apart. Email 1: insight-led cold open, light CTA. Email 2: case study or result, ask a specific question. Email 3: short breakup email — acknowledge they're busy, remove pressure, leave on a high note. Each email under 100 words."

Prompt 8: The Proposal Follow-Up

"Write a follow-up email to [prospect] who has had our proposal for [X] days and hasn't responded. Don't be pushy. Offer to answer any questions, remind them of the main outcome they'll get, and add a soft urgency element (limited availability, price validity). Under 80 words."

Prompt 9: The LinkedIn → Email Bridge

"I connected with [name] on LinkedIn and we had a brief exchange about [topic]. Now I want to move to email. Write an email that bridges from that LinkedIn conversation to a proper introduction, establishes credibility with a brief client result, and ends with a call invite. Under 100 words. Casual and direct."

Prompt 10: The Seasonal/Event-Based Email

"Write a timely outreach email I can send to cold prospects. The context: [industry event / time of year / news event]. Connect that context to a relevant pain point for [audience]. Offer something useful — an insight, a tool, or a quick audit. End with a CTA. Under 90 words."

Making AI Output Sound Like You

The first draft ChatGPT gives you is a starting point — not a finished email.

Edit for:

  • Your voice: Add a phrase you'd actually say. Remove phrases you'd never say.
  • Specificity: Replace generic lines with something specific to the prospect. Check their LinkedIn, recent company news, or their website.
  • Clarity: Cut any sentence that doesn't earn its place.

A good target: 80% AI-written, 20% you. The 20% is what makes it land.

A/B Test Your Prompts Like Subject Lines

Build two different prompts for the same email type. See which one produces better output. Document what worked.

Over time you'll have a playbook of prompts that consistently produce high-quality output for your specific business and audience.

This is the AI flywheel: better prompts → better emails → better results → data to improve prompts.

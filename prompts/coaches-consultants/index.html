15 ChatGPT Prompts for Coaches & Consultants That Fill Your Pipeline

Coaching and consulting businesses live or die on the quality of their positioning and outreach. These prompts help you articulate your value, convert more discovery calls, and create content that attracts ideal clients on autopilot.

Prompt 1 of 15
Positioning statement (elevator pitch)
Write a 2-sentence positioning statement for my coaching/consulting business. I help [TARGET CLIENT] who struggle with [PROBLEM] to achieve [OUTCOME] through [METHOD/APPROACH]. Make it specific, outcome-focused, and jargon-free. Generate 3 variations — ranging from bold/direct to empathetic/nurturing. I'll pick and refine.
💡 Pro tip: The best positioning statements describe the client's before state and after state in their own language, not yours.
Prompt 2 of 15
Discovery call script (opening 5 minutes)
Write the opening 5 minutes of a discovery call script for a [TYPE OF COACHING/CONSULTING] business. Cover: warm welcome, call agenda (2 sentences), transition question to understand their situation, and the key qualifying question. Conversational, not scripted-sounding. Focus on listening, not pitching.
💡 Pro tip: The first 5 minutes of a discovery call determines whether the prospect feels heard or sold to. Listen 80%, talk 20%.
Prompt 3 of 15
Proposal introduction (high-ticket)
Write the introduction section of a coaching/consulting proposal for [PROSPECT NAME/COMPANY]. Reference what I learned in our discovery call: their problem ([PROBLEM]), their goal ([GOAL]), and what's at stake if they don't solve it ([COST OF INACTION]). Then bridge to my solution in one sentence. Under 200 words. Tone: confident, empathetic.
💡 Pro tip: The best proposals make the client feel you deeply understood them before you pitch anything.
Prompt 4 of 15
Case study (client result)
Write a case study about a coaching/consulting client result. Client: [TYPE OF CLIENT, no name]. Challenge they faced: [CHALLENGE]. What we worked on: [PROCESS/APPROACH]. Result achieved: [RESULT with specifics]. Timeline: [TIMEFRAME]. Write as a narrative story, 200-250 words, with a pull-quote from the client ([QUOTE or 'generate a believable placeholder']). End with a CTA.
💡 Pro tip: Case studies with specific numbers (revenue increase, hours saved, score improvement) convert 3x better than vague outcome stories.
Prompt 5 of 15
Email sequence (lead magnet follow-up)
Write a 4-email nurture sequence for people who downloaded my [LEAD MAGNET TITLE]. Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the resource + set up what's coming. Email 2 (Day 2): One actionable insight from my framework. Email 3 (Day 5): A client story/case study. Email 4 (Day 8): Direct invitation to a discovery call. Each under 200 words. Tone: personal, value-first.
💡 Pro tip: Lead magnet sequences that deliver value before pitching convert at 4x the rate of immediate sales emails.
Prompt 6 of 15
LinkedIn post (contrarian take)
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a contrarian take about [COMMON BELIEF IN MY NICHE — e.g., 'more accountability isn't the answer']. Structure: 1-line hook that challenges the belief, 3 paragraphs explaining my perspective with examples, and a reframe that positions my approach as better. Under 1,300 characters. End with a question.
💡 Pro tip: Contrarian posts get the highest engagement in thought-leadership niches. Respectfully challenge, don't lecture.
Prompt 7 of 15
DM outreach (warm lead on social)
Write a DM message to [PROSPECT NAME] who engaged with my content ([SPECIFIC POST OR TOPIC]). Reference what they engaged with, ask a relevant question about their situation, and offer a resource or insight related to [YOUR TOPIC]. Do NOT pitch your program. Under 100 words. Warm, not spammy.
💡 Pro tip: DMs that reference specific content interactions get 5x more replies than generic outreach.
Prompt 8 of 15
Objection handler: 'The price is too high'
Write a response to a prospect who says 'I'd love to work with you but [PRICE] is too much.' Do NOT discount immediately. Instead: reframe around ROI (what's the cost of NOT solving this problem?), offer a payment plan option if available, and end with a question: 'What investment would feel right for you?' Under 150 words. Confident but empathetic.
💡 Pro tip: Discounting signals low confidence. Reframe value and offer a payment plan before reducing price.
Prompt 9 of 15
Testimonial request email
Write an email to a client [NAME] at the end of our engagement asking for a testimonial. Make the ask specific — provide 3 optional questions they can answer: (1) what was the situation before, (2) what changed, (3) who would you recommend this for. Include a Google/LinkedIn review link. Under 100 words. Low-friction ask.
💡 Pro tip: Providing specific questions makes testimonials 3x more likely to be detailed and useful.
Prompt 10 of 15
Workshop/webinar description (sales page)
Write a 250-word sales page description for a [LIVE WORKSHOP/WEBINAR] titled '[TITLE]' for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Cover: the problem they're experiencing, what they'll learn (3 outcomes), why this approach works (your unique method), logistics (date/time/format), and a CTA to register. Urgency element: [DEADLINE or LIMITED SPOTS]. Tone: direct, warm.
💡 Pro tip: The #1 mistake in workshop copy is describing topics instead of outcomes. Sell the transformation, not the curriculum.
Prompt 11 of 15
Onboarding welcome message (new client)
Write a welcome message to send to a new client [NAME] who just signed up for [PROGRAM/ENGAGEMENT]. Include: excitement about what's ahead, practical next steps (3 steps), how to reach me, and one mindset primer for the work we'll do. Under 200 words. Warm, energizing, organized.
💡 Pro tip: The onboarding experience determines early engagement. Clients who feel welcomed and organized are 50% more likely to complete the program.
Prompt 12 of 15
Content pillars (social media strategy)
Help me define 4 content pillars for my coaching/consulting business in [NICHE]. For each pillar, provide: the topic, the target emotion/pain point it addresses, 3 specific post ideas, and the desired business outcome (brand awareness / lead gen / authority). My ideal client: [IDEAL CLIENT]. My main offer: [OFFER].
💡 Pro tip: 4 content pillars give you enough variety to post daily without feeling scattered.
Prompt 13 of 15
Nurture email (inactive lead)
Write a re-engagement email to a lead [NAME] who showed interest in [PROGRAM] 3-6 months ago but didn't convert. Reference their original inquiry, acknowledge that timing may not have been right, share a brief case study or insight relevant to their situation, and invite them to reconnect. No hard sell. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: 60% of leads who don't convert initially will buy within 12 months if you stay in contact with value.
Prompt 14 of 15
Podcast pitch email (for guest appearances)
Write a pitch email to podcast host [HOST NAME] of '[PODCAST NAME]' proposing me as a guest. Cover: who I am in 2 sentences, why I'm relevant to their audience (reference a specific episode), and my proposed topic with 3 specific talking points. End with a clear ask: 'Would you be open to a 15-minute pre-interview call?' Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Reference a specific episode the host will remember. It proves you actually listened.
Prompt 15 of 15
Cold email (ideal client outreach)
Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME], a [TITLE] at [COMPANY], who fits my ideal client profile. Lead with a specific observation about a challenge they likely face ([CHALLENGE]). Offer a relevant insight or quick win. Propose a 20-minute call to share a framework that's helped similar [CLIENT TYPE]. CTA is low-friction. Under 125 words.
💡 Pro tip: Personalized cold emails that lead with the prospect's specific challenge (not your credentials) get 3x higher reply rates.

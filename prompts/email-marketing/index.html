12 ChatGPT Email Marketing Prompts That Drive Opens and Clicks

Email still delivers a 36:1 ROI — higher than any other channel. These prompts help you write subject lines that get opened, sequences that nurture, and promos that sell.

Prompt 1 of 5
Subject line generator (10 variations)
Write 10 email subject lines for an email about "[EMAIL TOPIC/OFFER]". Include: 2 curiosity-gap subjects, 2 benefit-driven subjects, 2 urgency/scarcity subjects, 2 personalization subjects (using "[First Name]"), and 2 question subjects. Max 45 characters each. No clickbait — every subject must be deliverable by the email body.
💡 Pro tip: A/B test subject lines every send. Over 20 sends, this alone can double open rates.
Prompt 2 of 5
Welcome email sequence (3-email series)
Write a 3-email welcome sequence for new subscribers who signed up for [LEAD MAGNET/OFFER]. Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the promised resource + introduce who I am. Email 2 (Day 2): Share the biggest insight/mistake in [NICHE] + soft pitch to [PRODUCT]. Email 3 (Day 4): Social proof (testimonial or result) + direct CTA to [PRODUCT/OFFER]. Tone: [TONE]. Keep each email under 250 words.
💡 Pro tip: The welcome sequence has 5x higher open rates than regular emails. Front-load your best content here.
Prompt 3 of 5
Abandoned cart recovery email
Write an abandoned cart email for someone who viewed [PRODUCT] but didn't purchase. Include: a subject line with the product name, acknowledgment of their interest without being pushy, the top 3 reasons to buy (benefits, not features), a clear CTA button, and a P.S. that adds social proof or urgency. Under 200 words. Tone: helpful, not desperate.
💡 Pro tip: Abandoned cart emails sent within 1 hour of abandonment recover 3x more sales than those sent later.
Prompt 4 of 5
Promotional email (launch/offer)
Write a promotional email announcing [OFFER/LAUNCH] to my email list. Structure: headline that leads with the benefit, 1 paragraph of context/story, 3 bullet points of what they get, price and any scarcity, primary CTA. Tone: excited but not salesy. Include a subject line and preview text. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: The P.S. in promo emails often outperforms the main CTA. Add a second link there.
Prompt 5 of 5
Re-engagement campaign (inactive subscribers)
Write a 2-email re-engagement sequence for subscribers who haven't opened in 90 days. Email 1: Acknowledge they haven't heard from me, ask if they still want to be on the list, offer an incentive to re-engage ([INCENTIVE]). Email 2 (3 days later, only to non-openers): Final warning they'll be removed, make re-subscribing easy, light urgency. Both should be under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Cleaning your list of inactive subscribers improves deliverability for everyone else. Don't be afraid to cut.

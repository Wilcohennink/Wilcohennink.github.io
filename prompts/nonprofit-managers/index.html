15 ChatGPT Prompts for Nonprofit Managers That Raise More and Inspire Action

Nonprofits change the world through compelling stories and strategic communication. These prompts help you write the donation appeals, grant proposals, and donor relationships that fund your mission — without a full communications team.

Prompt 1 of 15
Year-end donation appeal email
Write a year-end donation appeal email for [NONPROFIT NAME] supporting [CAUSE]. Include: a subject line with emotional urgency, an opening story about one person/family/community affected by the work, the specific impact of [DONATION AMOUNT] (e.g., 'feeds a family for a week'), a tax deadline reminder (Dec 31), and a primary donation CTA with a link. Under 300 words. Story-first, mission-centered.
💡 Pro tip: Donation appeals with a single, specific beneficiary story outperform statistics-led appeals by 300%. One face beats a thousand data points.
Prompt 2 of 15
Grant proposal executive summary
Write an executive summary for a grant proposal to [FUNDER NAME] requesting [AMOUNT] for [PROJECT/PROGRAM NAME]. Include: the problem being addressed (specific, local data if possible), proposed solution, expected outcomes (3 measurable metrics), organization's credibility (brief), and the request. Under 300 words. Compelling, evidence-based.
💡 Pro tip: Grant readers review 50+ proposals. An executive summary that names the specific problem, solution, and measurable impact in the first paragraph wins more grants.
Prompt 3 of 15
Donor thank-you letter (first-time donor)
Write a thank-you letter to a first-time donor [NAME] who gave [$AMOUNT] to [NONPROFIT NAME]. Include: immediate gratitude, the specific impact their gift will have (connected to a program), a brief story that brings the mission to life, and a soft close that cultivates the relationship (no additional ask). Under 200 words. Warm, specific, relationship-building.
💡 Pro tip: First-time donors who receive a personal thank-you within 48 hours are 4x more likely to give again.
Prompt 4 of 15
Volunteer recruitment post (social media)
Write a social media post recruiting volunteers for [NONPROFIT NAME]. Cover: what volunteers do (specific roles), when and where ([SCHEDULE/LOCATION/REMOTE OPTION]), the impact of volunteering (1-2 sentences), and a CTA to apply or learn more via [LINK]. Platform: [Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn]. Under 200 words. Inspiring, specific, accessible.
💡 Pro tip: Volunteer posts that describe a specific role ('help sort donations on Saturday mornings') attract more sign-ups than vague 'we need volunteers!' posts.
Prompt 5 of 15
Impact report narrative (annual)
Write the narrative introduction for [NONPROFIT NAME]'s annual impact report for [YEAR]. Cover: a brief summary of the year in mission terms, 3-4 key achievements with metrics, a story that illustrates the human impact, a thank-you to donors and partners, and a look forward to [NEXT YEAR]. Under 400 words. Inspiring, data-grounded.
💡 Pro tip: Annual reports that lead with a story and follow with data engage donors more deeply than those that lead with financial statements.
Prompt 6 of 15
Corporate sponsorship proposal (email)
Write an email to [COMPANY NAME] proposing a corporate sponsorship of [EVENT/PROGRAM] at [NONPROFIT NAME]. Cover: brief description of the nonprofit and mission, the sponsorship opportunity (what the event/program is), sponsorship benefits for the company ([LOGO PLACEMENT/NAMING/EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT/TAX DEDUCTION]), and a tiered ask ($[X], $[Y], $[Z]). Under 250 words. CTA: call to discuss.
💡 Pro tip: Corporate sponsorship proposals that lead with employee engagement benefits (not just logo exposure) convert better with CSR-focused companies.
Prompt 7 of 15
Major donor outreach email (cold introduction)
Write an introductory email to a potential major donor [NAME] about [NONPROFIT NAME]. They have indicated interest in [CAUSE] through [CONNECTION — event attendance, board recommendation, public giving history]. Lead with the impact the organization creates, not the ask. Invite a 30-minute discovery call. Under 150 words. Relational, not transactional.
💡 Pro tip: Major donors who receive a story-led introduction (impact first) before any ask convert at 3x the rate of those who receive a pitch-first email.
Prompt 8 of 15
Recurring giving program launch email
Write an email to existing donors launching [NONPROFIT NAME]'s monthly giving program '[PROGRAM NAME]'. Explain: what monthly giving means for mission continuity, the specific impact of a [AMOUNT]/month gift, what monthly givers receive in return (updates, recognition, insider access), and a CTA to sign up. Under 200 words. Cultivate, don't push.
💡 Pro tip: Monthly giving programs improve donor retention from 45% to 85%. The launch email to existing donors is your highest-conversion channel.
Prompt 9 of 15
Event invitation email (fundraising gala/run)
Write an event invitation email for [NONPROFIT NAME]'s [EVENT NAME] on [DATE] at [LOCATION]. Include: why this event matters (mission connection), what to expect (agenda highlights, 2-3 bullet points), ticket/registration price, and a CTA to register with a link. Create genuine excitement — this is a celebration, not just a fundraiser. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Fundraising event invitations that frame the event as a celebration of impact (not just a fundraising opportunity) convert better and attract mission-aligned guests.
Prompt 10 of 15
Newsletter (donor update, monthly)
Write a monthly donor newsletter for [NONPROFIT NAME]. Include: 1 mission story (specific, human), 1 program milestone or metric achieved, a 'did you know?' fact about the cause, and a brief look at what's coming next month. Under 300 words. Donors should feel informed, appreciated, and connected — not asked for money.
💡 Pro tip: Donor newsletters that never ask for money in every issue retain donors 40% longer than those that always include an appeal.
Prompt 11 of 15
Volunteer appreciation post (social media)
Write a social media post appreciating and recognizing volunteers at [NONPROFIT NAME] during [VOLUNTEER APPRECIATION WEEK / specific program]. Be specific about what they do, the impact they create, and express genuine organizational gratitude. Tag your platform's volunteer community if applicable. Under 200 words. Heartfelt.
💡 Pro tip: Public volunteer recognition drives volunteer retention and new volunteer applications simultaneously — recognition is recruitment.
Prompt 12 of 15
Foundation grant LOI (Letter of Inquiry)
Write a Letter of Inquiry to [FOUNDATION NAME] for a potential grant to [NONPROFIT NAME]. Cover: organization overview (2 sentences), the problem addressed, proposed project summary (3-4 sentences), expected impact (2-3 metrics), funding request (amount and general use), and why this fits the foundation's priorities ([FOUNDATION'S STATED PRIORITIES]). Under 350 words. Concise, aligned with funder priorities.
💡 Pro tip: LOIs that mirror the foundation's exact language from their grant guidelines score significantly higher in initial reviews.
Prompt 13 of 15
Donor stewardship phone call script
Write a 3-minute phone call script for a donor stewardship call to [DONOR NAME] who gave [$AMOUNT] to [NONPROFIT NAME]. Opener: warm thank-you, mission update (1 specific story), impact of their gift (tied to a program), soft relational close (no ask). The goal is to make them feel valued — not to solicit another gift. Natural, conversational.
💡 Pro tip: Stewardship calls that don't include an ask increase the likelihood of the next unsolicited gift by 200%. The relationship IS the ask.
Prompt 14 of 15
Online fundraising campaign copy (crowdfunding)
Write copy for an online fundraising campaign on [GoFundMe/Classy/Network for Good] for [NONPROFIT NAME]. Campaign: [CAMPAIGN NAME]. Include: campaign headline (goal + heart), a 300-word story, the specific ask ($[GOAL]), a breakdown of how funds will be used (3-4 line items), social sharing encouragement, and donor impact statement (what $25/$50/$100 does). Urgent, human, specific.
💡 Pro tip: Crowdfunding campaigns with a specific, vivid impact breakdown ('$50 buys 3 months of tutoring for one child') raise 40% more than those with general fund descriptions.
Prompt 15 of 15
Staff/board member bio (website)
Write a professional bio for [NAME], [TITLE] at [NONPROFIT NAME]. Include: their role and responsibilities, relevant background (prior experience, education), why they're passionate about [CAUSE] (1 personal sentence), and any notable accomplishments in the sector. Under 200 words. Warm, mission-connected, professional.
💡 Pro tip: Nonprofit staff bios that include a personal connection to the mission build donor trust more than credential-only bios.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Amazon Sellers That Rank Higher and Convert Better

Amazon is a search engine powered by keywords and conversions. These prompts help you write listings that rank, images that convert, and campaigns that drive profitable sales.

Prompt 1 of 15
Amazon product title (SEO-optimized)
Write 3 Amazon product title variations for [PRODUCT NAME]. Category: [CATEGORY]. Key features: [LIST KEY FEATURES]. Main keywords: [LIST 3-5 KEYWORDS]. Follow Amazon's title guidelines: lead with brand name, include primary keyword, key attributes (size/color/count), and stay under 200 characters. No promotional language or symbols.
💡 Pro tip: Amazon's A9 algorithm indexes the title most heavily. Lead with your highest-volume keyword after the brand name.
Prompt 2 of 15
Bullet points (5 benefits)
Write 5 Amazon bullet points for [PRODUCT NAME]. Transform these features into customer benefits: [LIST 5 FEATURES]. Format each bullet: start with an ALL-CAPS benefit title, followed by the feature-as-benefit explanation (2-3 sentences). Include 1-2 relevant keywords per bullet naturally. Each bullet under 250 characters.
💡 Pro tip: Bullets that start with the benefit (not the feature) increase add-to-cart rates by 15-20%. Customers scan bullets in under 10 seconds.
Prompt 3 of 15
Product description / A+ content body
Write an Amazon product description or A+ content body for [PRODUCT NAME]. Target customer: [AVATAR]. Key use cases: [LIST 3]. Pain points solved: [LIST 3]. Include: an engaging opener, a feature/benefit breakdown, a use case scenario, a social proof signal ('over X customers'), and a closing CTA to 'Add to Cart'. Under 350 words. Keyword-rich, natural.
💡 Pro tip: A+ content increases conversion rates by 3-10% vs. standard descriptions. It's the highest-ROI listing optimization on Amazon.
Prompt 4 of 15
Backend keyword list (search terms)
Generate a backend search term keyword list for an Amazon listing for [PRODUCT NAME] in the [CATEGORY] category. Include: 5 long-tail keywords, 5 competitor brand-adjacent terms (generic alternatives, not brand names), 5 use-case keywords, and 5 audience/demographic keywords. Format for Amazon backend field (space-separated, under 250 bytes total, no repetition of front-end title keywords).
💡 Pro tip: Backend keywords capture searches your title can't fit. Focus on long-tail and misspellings — these have lower competition.
Prompt 5 of 15
Product launch email (to existing customers)
Write an email to [BRAND NAME]'s existing customer list announcing the launch of [NEW PRODUCT NAME] on Amazon. Include: what's new, the key benefit, launch price or promotion (if any), and a direct Amazon link. Ask for honest reviews in exchange for trying the product at a discount (compliant with Amazon's ToS — discount via Seller Central, no reviews-for-incentives). Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Early review velocity signals relevance to Amazon's algorithm. Legitimate launch campaigns from your email list are the most effective way to build it.
Prompt 6 of 15
Review response (positive)
Write a response to this positive Amazon review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Thank the reviewer, acknowledge what they loved, add one piece of useful product information, and invite them to contact [BRAND NAME] customer service with any questions. Under 100 words. Warm, brand-reinforcing.
💡 Pro tip: Amazon review responses are public — they signal to future buyers that there's a real brand behind the product.
Prompt 7 of 15
Review response (critical/negative)
Write a response to this critical Amazon review: '[PASTE REVIEW]'. Acknowledge the issue without being defensive, apologize for the experience, explain what might have caused it (if known), and offer to resolve via your customer service contact. Do NOT offer refunds in the public review — handle offline. Under 100 words. Professional and empathetic.
💡 Pro tip: Critical review responses that offer to resolve the issue offline convert reading prospects better than reviews left unanswered.
Prompt 8 of 15
PPC campaign ad headline variations
Write 10 Amazon Sponsored Product ad headline variations for [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: 2 problem-solution headlines, 2 comparison headlines ('Better than [generic alternative]'), 2 benefit-led headlines, 2 urgency/scarcity headlines, and 2 social proof headlines. Max 150 characters each. No competitor brand names.
💡 Pro tip: Testing 5+ ad headline variations in your first campaign reveals which messaging angle resonates — data that improves all your copy.
Prompt 9 of 15
Brand story (Amazon brand registry)
Write an Amazon Brand Story section for [BRAND NAME]. Cover: the founder story or brand origin (1-2 sentences), the problem the brand was founded to solve, what makes your products different, and your brand values or commitment to customers. 150-250 words. Authentic, not corporate. For the 'From the brand' section on Amazon listings.
💡 Pro tip: Brand stories increase trust with buyers who would otherwise buy the cheapest similar product. They're especially important in competitive categories.
Prompt 10 of 15
Q&A response (Amazon customer questions)
Write accurate, helpful responses to these customer questions about [PRODUCT NAME]: [LIST 2-3 COMMON QUESTIONS — e.g., 'Is this compatible with X?', 'What material is it made of?', 'Does it come with Y?']. Each answer: specific, complete, and under 100 words. Include the key selling point or reassurance where relevant.
💡 Pro tip: Proactively answering common questions (even before customers ask) reduces decision friction and prevents negative reviews from confused buyers.
Prompt 11 of 15
Seller feedback request email (post-delivery)
Write a post-purchase email template requesting seller feedback from a customer who received [PRODUCT NAME]. Thank them for the purchase, ask if everything arrived in good condition, and invite them to leave seller feedback via their order. No incentives or review requests — seller feedback only. Under 100 words. Compliant with Amazon ToS.
💡 Pro tip: Seller feedback emails are different from product review requests — they're fully ToS-compliant and protect your seller account health.
Prompt 12 of 15
Deal/coupon announcement (social media)
Write a social media post announcing an Amazon deal or coupon for [PRODUCT NAME]. Include: product name, the discount ([%] off or [$X] off with coupon code [CODE]), the Amazon link, and a brief product description. Platform: [Facebook group / Instagram / Twitter]. Create light urgency (limited time/stock). Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Amazon product posts in relevant Facebook groups (buy-local, deals groups, niche communities) can generate significant sales velocity during launch.
Prompt 13 of 15
Supplier outreach email (Alibaba sourcing)
Write an email to a potential Alibaba supplier for [PRODUCT CATEGORY]. Include: introduction of your business ([BRIEF DESCRIPTION]), the product specifications ([LIST KEY SPECS]), order quantity ([MOQ/FIRST ORDER SIZE]), requested samples (1 unit for testing), lead time inquiry, and packaging requirement. Professional, specific. Request their price sheet and catalog. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Specific product specification emails get faster, more accurate quotes than vague 'I'm interested in your products' messages.
Prompt 14 of 15
FBA shipment issue escalation email
Write an email to Amazon Seller Support escalating an FBA issue: [DESCRIBE ISSUE — e.g., lost inventory, stranded listing, reimbursement request]. Include: case reference (if any), order/shipment ID, specific impact (units lost, revenue affected), what resolution you're requesting, and a professional but firm tone. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Amazon support escalations with specific case numbers, financial impact, and a clear resolution request get resolved 2x faster than vague complaints.
Prompt 15 of 15
Amazon storefront copy (brand store)
Write copy for the main page of [BRAND NAME]'s Amazon Brand Store. Include: a hero section tagline (under 15 words), brand mission statement (2 sentences), 3 product collection headlines with 1-sentence descriptions, and a brand values section (3 bullet points). Under 300 words total. SEO-friendly: include '[PRODUCT CATEGORY]' keywords naturally.
💡 Pro tip: Amazon Brand Stores that clearly communicate the brand's niche ('premium outdoor cooking accessories for serious grillers') convert 40% better than generic stores.

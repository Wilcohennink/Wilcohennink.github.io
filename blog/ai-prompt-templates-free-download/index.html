Free AI Prompt Templates for Business (15 Ready-to-Use Prompts)

No sign-up required. Just copy, paste, and use.

These 15 prompt templates cover the most common business tasks. They're written to be specific enough to produce useful output — not generic enough to produce garbage.

How to Use These Templates

1. Copy the prompt

2. Replace the text in [brackets] with your specifics

3. Paste into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI

4. Edit the output — it's a draft, not a final product

Email Templates

Template 1: Professional Email Response

"You are a [professional role] at [company type]. Write a professional email response to the following message: [paste email]. Respond to every point raised. Keep it concise. Tone: [formal/friendly/direct]. Under 150 words."

Template 2: Meeting Request Email

"Write a meeting request email to [recipient's role] about [meeting topic]. Context: [1-2 sentences about why you're reaching out]. Offer 2-3 time slots: [paste your availability]. Keep it short and direct. Under 80 words."

Template 3: Apology Email to a Client

"Write a professional apology email to a client after [describe the issue]. Acknowledge what happened without over-explaining or making excuses. Explain what you're doing to fix it. Offer [compensation if any]. Keep it sincere and under 120 words."

Marketing Templates

Template 4: Social Media Post (LinkedIn)

"Write a LinkedIn post about [topic]. Target audience: [audience]. My perspective/take: [1-2 sentences]. Format: Start with a hook (short, bold statement). 3-4 short paragraphs. End with a question to drive engagement. Max 200 words. No hashtags unless natural."

Template 5: Email Newsletter

"Write a weekly email newsletter for [business type] about [topic]. Structure: 1-paragraph intro, 3 tips with brief explanations (2-3 sentences each), 1 CTA at the end. Tone: direct, human, not corporate. Under 400 words."

Template 6: Product Description

"Write a product description for [product name]. What it does: [describe]. Key features: [list 3-4]. Target customer: [who it's for]. Tone: confident and clear. Under 100 words. End with the price and a CTA."

Template 7: Blog Post Outline

"Create a detailed outline for a blog post titled '[title]'. Include: intro hook, 5-6 main sections (with H2 headings and 3 bullet points each), and a conclusion with CTA. Target keyword: [keyword]. The reader should finish with a clear understanding of [main takeaway]."

Sales Templates

Template 8: Cold Outreach Email

"Write a cold email to a [job title] at a [company type]. I offer [what you do/sell]. Their likely challenge: [pain point]. Keep it under 90 words. Don't open with 'I hope this finds you well.' End with a low-friction CTA — a 15-minute call, not 'let me know if you're interested.'"

Template 9: Proposal Summary Section

"Write an executive summary for a proposal to [type of client] for [service/project]. Their goal: [goal]. Our proposed solution: [brief]. Expected outcome: [result]. Budget: [if relevant]. Keep it under 150 words. Professional and outcome-focused."

Template 10: Follow-Up Email

"Write a follow-up email to [name] who hasn't replied to my email about [topic] sent [X] days ago. Don't apologise for following up. Add one new piece of value — a relevant stat, insight, or short case study. End with a single, specific question. Under 70 words."

Operations Templates

Template 11: SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

"Write a step-by-step SOP for [process name]. Audience: new team member with no prior knowledge. Include: purpose, tools needed, step-by-step instructions (numbered), common mistakes to avoid, and what to do if something goes wrong. Clear and jargon-free."

Template 12: Job Description

"Write a job description for a [role title] at a [company type] startup. Responsibilities: [list 4-5]. Requirements: [list must-haves]. Nice to haves: [list 2-3]. Company culture in one sentence: [describe]. Tone: professional but not stuffy. Include what makes this role exciting."

Template 13: Weekly Recap Email to Manager

"Write a weekly recap email from [my role] to my manager covering the week of [date]. Include: top 3 accomplishments, what's in progress, any blockers, and priorities for next week. Format as short bullets, not paragraphs. Professional but not robotic."

Customer Service Templates

Template 14: Complaint Response

"Write a professional response to this customer complaint: '[paste complaint]'. Acknowledge the issue and how they must feel. Apologise once, sincerely, without over-explaining. State exactly what you'll do and when. Offer [resolution/discount/next steps]. Keep it under 120 words."

Template 15: FAQ Response Template

"Write a clear, helpful response to this customer question: '[paste question]'. Our answer is [paste your answer]. Rewrite it so it's easy to understand, complete, and friendly. Add a next-step suggestion if relevant. Under 80 words."

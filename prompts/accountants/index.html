15 ChatGPT Prompts for Accountants That Build Client Trust

Accountants who communicate proactively retain more clients and win more referrals. These prompts help you write the newsletters, advisory emails, and marketing content that position you as the go-to advisor — not just the tax preparer.

Prompt 1 of 15
Client newsletter (tax season prep)
Write a short client newsletter section preparing clients for [UPCOMING TAX SEASON/QUARTER]. Include: 3 documents they should be gathering, 1 common deduction they often miss, and 1 upcoming deadline with date. Plain English, no jargon. Under 250 words. Add a CTA to schedule their appointment via [LINK/PHONE].
💡 Pro tip: Pre-season newsletters reduce last-minute client scrambles and improve the quality of documents you receive.
Prompt 2 of 15
LinkedIn post (tax tip)
Write a LinkedIn post sharing one actionable tax tip for [TARGET AUDIENCE — small business owners, freelancers, etc.]. Format: 1-line hook, 2-3 paragraphs explaining the tip in plain English with an example, and a closing CTA to DM for a consultation. Under 1,200 characters. No jargon. Add a compliance disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Educational tax tips shared on LinkedIn are the highest-converting content type for accountants building an advisory practice.
Prompt 3 of 15
Advisory email (year-end planning)
Write a year-end tax planning email to send to small business clients in [OCTOBER/NOVEMBER]. Cover: 3 actions they should take before December 31 (e.g., max retirement contributions, review equipment purchases, defer income), why each matters, and an invitation to schedule a planning call. Under 300 words. Proactive, not reactive.
💡 Pro tip: Year-end planning emails sent in October/November generate more billable advisory hours than any other client communication.
Prompt 4 of 15
Proposal (new client onboarding)
Write a new client proposal for [SERVICE TYPE — bookkeeping/tax prep/CFO services] for [BUSINESS TYPE]. Include: understanding of their situation, what's included (deliverables), engagement timeline, fee structure ([MONTHLY/ANNUAL/HOURLY]), communication expectations, and next steps. Under 400 words. Professional, clear, confidence-building.
💡 Pro tip: Proposals that describe what the client has been struggling with (before pitching your solution) have 60% higher acceptance rates.
Prompt 5 of 15
Cold email (small business owner)
Write a cold email to [PROSPECT NAME], owner of [BUSINESS TYPE] in [CITY]. Lead with a common financial problem in their industry ([PROBLEM — e.g., 'most [industry] businesses overpay in [tax area]']). Offer a specific, low-risk first step ([FREE REVIEW/CALL]). Under 150 words. No attachments in the first email.
💡 Pro tip: Industry-specific cold emails (showing you know their specific tax situation) get 3x more responses than generic outreach.
Prompt 6 of 15
Service page copy (accounting firm website)
Write website service page copy for a [SERVICE — bookkeeping / tax / payroll / advisory] practice targeting [TARGET CLIENT]. Include: headline (outcome-focused, not 'we provide'), the problem this service solves, what's included (5 bullet points), who it's for, a differentiator from DIY options (like TurboTax), and a CTA. Under 400 words.
💡 Pro tip: Service pages that contrast with DIY alternatives ('unlike TurboTax, we...') convert better with cost-conscious small business owners.
Prompt 7 of 15
S-Corp election explanation email
Write a client-friendly email explaining what an S-Corp election is and whether it might make sense for their business. Cover: what it is in simple terms, the potential tax savings (example: for a business earning [INCOME] it could save roughly [RANGE]), the requirements and risks, and an invitation to discuss. Under 300 words. Add a disclaimer that this is general information.
💡 Pro tip: S-Corp conversations are one of the highest-value advisory interactions you can have with growing sole proprietors — proactively raising it builds trust.
Prompt 8 of 15
Referral partner email (to attorney/financial advisor)
Write an email to [ATTORNEY/FINANCIAL ADVISOR] at [FIRM] proposing a referral partnership. Explain who my ideal clients are ([CLIENT TYPE]), who their ideal clients are ([THEIR TYPE]), and why the overlap creates mutual value. Propose a coffee meeting. Under 150 words. Professional, mutual-value framing.
💡 Pro tip: Attorney and financial advisor referral partnerships are the highest-quality lead sources for most accounting firms.
Prompt 9 of 15
Quarterly check-in email (business clients)
Write a quarterly check-in email to send to business clients at the start of [Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4]. Include: a brief summary of what this quarter typically involves (estimated taxes, payroll deadlines, etc.), 2 questions to prompt a conversation with you, and a CTA to schedule a brief call if they have questions. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Quarterly check-ins position you as a proactive advisor and reduce end-of-year surprises for clients.
Prompt 10 of 15
FAQ for website (common client questions)
Write answers to 5 FAQ questions for an accounting firm's website serving [NICHE — small businesses/freelancers/real estate investors]. Questions: (1) How much do your services cost? (2) What's the difference between a bookkeeper and a CPA? (3) When should I hire an accountant vs. DIY? (4) What records do I need to keep? (5) Can you help if I'm behind on taxes? Clear, plain English, under 75 words each.
💡 Pro tip: FAQ pages that answer the real objections (cost, DIY vs. hire, behind on taxes) convert fence-sitters better than any service page.
Prompt 11 of 15
End-of-engagement summary email
Write an email to client [NAME] at the end of a tax engagement, summarizing: what was filed/completed, key numbers ([REFUND/LIABILITY/BALANCE]), any action items they need to take, and next steps for the year ahead. Under 200 words. Professional, clear, relationship-sustaining.
💡 Pro tip: End-of-engagement summaries reduce follow-up calls by 50% and increase client satisfaction scores.
Prompt 12 of 15
Price increase announcement
Write an email to existing clients announcing a fee increase from [OLD RATE] to [NEW RATE] effective [DATE]. Frame it around the value delivered and costs of maintaining quality service. Give adequate notice (60+ days). Thank them for their continued trust. No apology for charging appropriate fees. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Accountants who bundle the price increase announcement with a specific service enhancement (new software, added advisory call) see 70% retention vs. 50% without it.
Prompt 13 of 15
Google Business post (tax deadline reminder)
Write a Google Business Profile post reminding local businesses about the upcoming [TAX DEADLINE] on [DATE]. Include a brief tip on what to have ready, and a CTA to call or book an appointment before spots fill. Under 150 words. Keyword-friendly: include '[CITY] accountant' naturally.
💡 Pro tip: Google Business posts around deadlines drive the highest local search visibility and call volume for accounting firms.
Prompt 14 of 15
Client onboarding checklist email
Write a client onboarding email for a new [BOOKKEEPING/TAX/ADVISORY] client at [FIRM NAME]. Include a checklist of what they need to provide in the first 2 weeks: [DOCUMENTS/ACCESS/INFO], how they'll get organized (your process), who their point of contact is, and expected timelines. Under 250 words. Organized, reassuring.
💡 Pro tip: A clear onboarding checklist sets professional expectations and reduces the back-and-forth that erodes client confidence.
Prompt 15 of 15
Case study (client win)
Write a brief case study about a client result for [FIRM NAME]. Client: [INDUSTRY, no name]. Challenge: [CHALLENGE]. What we did: [SOLUTION]. Result: [SPECIFIC RESULT — tax savings amount, time saved, growth enabled]. 200-250 words. For use on website and LinkedIn. End with a CTA to discuss similar results.
💡 Pro tip: Case studies with specific dollar amounts saved or earned convert readers to inquiries at 5x the rate of generic service descriptions.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Travel Agents That Book More Trips

The best travel agents win on knowledge, relationships, and the ability to make travel feel effortless. These prompts help you write the proposals, emails, and social content that position you as the indispensable expert clients call before they search online.

Prompt 1 of 15
Custom trip proposal (client-facing)
Write a custom travel proposal for [CLIENT NAME] for a [TRIP TYPE — honeymoon, family vacation, adventure trip] to [DESTINATION(S)] for [DURATION] in [MONTH/YEAR]. Budget: approximately [BUDGET]. Include: trip overview and theme, day-by-day highlights (not a minute-by-minute itinerary), accommodation description (style/standards), key experiences included, what's included vs. not, and the investment summary. Under 500 words. Evocative, aspirational.
💡 Pro tip: Travel proposals that describe experiences ('a private boat at sunrise') convert better than those that list hotel names and flight times.
Prompt 2 of 15
Destination spotlight email (newsletter)
Write a destination spotlight email for [DESTINATION] to send to your travel newsletter subscribers. Include: why this destination is compelling right now (trend/season/value), 3 unique experiences available there, best time to visit and current travel considerations, and a CTA to book a consultation for a custom trip. Under 300 words. Inspiring, informative.
💡 Pro tip: Destination spotlight emails sent during seasonal interest peaks (Europe in spring, ski destinations in October) have the highest conversion to consultation bookings.
Prompt 3 of 15
Client follow-up email (after inquiry call)
Write a follow-up email to [CLIENT NAME] after an initial trip consultation call. Summarize what you discussed: destination ([DESTINATION]), travel dates ([DATES]), group size ([GROUP]), and key priorities ([PRIORITIES]). Outline your proposed research approach and when they can expect a full proposal ([TIMEFRAME]). Under 200 words. Professional, organized, excited.
💡 Pro tip: Follow-up emails sent within 2 hours of the inquiry call have 50% higher response rates. Speed signals professionalism and excitement.
Prompt 4 of 15
Instagram caption (destination travel photo)
Write 3 Instagram caption variations for a stunning travel photo from [DESTINATION]. Version 1: experiential (what it felt like to be there). Version 2: practical tip (when to visit, what to know). Version 3: aspiration hook (makes followers want to be there immediately). 5 relevant travel hashtags for each. Under 150 words each.
💡 Pro tip: Travel posts with a single strong experiential detail ('the water was so clear you could see 20 feet down') outperform generic 'paradise found' captions 4:1 in saves.
Prompt 5 of 15
Group trip announcement (social media)
Write a social media post announcing a group trip or travel experience organized by [AGENCY/AGENT NAME] to [DESTINATION] on [DATES]. Include: where you're going and why it's special, who it's ideal for, what's included, pricing starting at [$X], and how to register or inquire. Create genuine interest and urgency (limited spots). Under 250 words.
💡 Pro tip: Group trip posts that describe the ideal participant ('for women who travel solo but want company for one epic trip') attract more qualified sign-ups than generic travel posts.
Prompt 6 of 15
Luxury travel email (high-net-worth prospect)
Write an email to a high-net-worth prospect [NAME] who was referred by [REFERRAL]. Lead with the quality of the travel experience, not price. Describe 1-2 specific luxury experiences in [THEIR INTEREST DESTINATION] that most travelers never access. Invite a conversation to design a custom trip. Under 150 words. Refined, specific, unrushed.
💡 Pro tip: Luxury travel clients are sold on access (places and experiences unavailable to most) — not discounts or inclusions lists.
Prompt 7 of 15
Post-trip follow-up email
Write a post-trip follow-up email to client [NAME] who just returned from [DESTINATION]. Ask about their experience, invite them to share any highlights or feedback, remind them of photos or details to collect ([FORMS/RECEIPTS/MEMORIES]), and plant a seed for their next adventure. Under 150 words. Warm, relationship-building.
💡 Pro tip: Post-trip follow-up emails sent within 3 days of return have the highest conversion to future booking conversations.
Prompt 8 of 15
Objection handler: 'I can book it myself online'
Write a response to a prospect who says 'I can book this trip myself on [TripAdvisor/Booking.com/Google].' Acknowledge the tools available, then explain 3 specific value-adds a travel agent provides that online tools can't ([INSIDER ACCESS, PRICE MATCHING, SUPPORT WHEN THINGS GO WRONG, ITINERARY OPTIMIZATION]). Offer a free consultation to show the difference. Under 150 words. Confident, not defensive.
💡 Pro tip: The 'I can do it myself' objection is beaten by specificity — 'I have private villa access that isn't on any booking site' beats 'I save you time'.
Prompt 9 of 15
Cold email (corporate travel prospect)
Write a cold email to [COMPANY NAME]'s office manager or executive assistant proposing your corporate travel management services. Lead with a common corporate travel pain point ([OVERSPENDING/LAST-MINUTE BOOKINGS/POLICY COMPLIANCE]). Explain how you solve it. Offer a free corporate travel audit. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Corporate travel prospects respond to cost and compliance angles — these are their bosses' KPIs. Lead with what you'll save them.
Prompt 10 of 15
Honeymoon package description
Write a honeymoon package description for [DESTINATION] for travel agency [NAME]. Include: destination overview and why it's perfect for honeymooners, accommodation options (2 styles: romance-focused), 3 included experiences, practical travel details (flight time, best months), pricing starting at [$X], and a CTA to personalize the package. Under 300 words. Romantic, aspirational.
💡 Pro tip: Honeymoon package descriptions that reference couple-specific experiences ('private beach dinners, sunrise yoga for two') outperform generic resort descriptions.
Prompt 11 of 15
Travel newsletter subject lines (A/B test)
Write 8 travel email newsletter subject line options for the topic [DESTINATION/PROMOTION/SEASON]. Include: 2 curiosity hooks, 2 urgency-based, 2 aspiration-based, and 2 practical/deal-based. Max 45 characters each. No clickbait — each subject must be deliverable by the email body.
💡 Pro tip: Travel email subject lines with a specific destination or experience name outperform generic 'Your next adventure awaits' lines by 3x.
Prompt 12 of 15
Travel insurance recommendation email
Write an email to client [NAME] recommending travel insurance for their upcoming trip to [DESTINATION]. Explain: why this trip specifically warrants coverage (specific risks — [WEATHER/HEALTH/POLITICAL/CANCELLATION]), what's typically covered, the cost vs. the potential loss, and how to proceed to add coverage. Under 200 words. Advisory tone — genuinely looking out for them.
💡 Pro tip: Travel agents who proactively recommend insurance build trust and protect their own reputation when things go wrong.
Prompt 13 of 15
Seasonal promotion email (flash sale)
Write a flash sale email for [AGENCY NAME] promoting [SPECIFIC DESTINATION/DEPARTURE] at [DISCOUNT/VALUE OFFER]. Include: subject line with urgency, 2-sentence destination hook, the offer details (dates, price, what's included), expiry date, and a clear CTA. Under 200 words. Urgency-driven, clear.
💡 Pro tip: Travel flash sale emails with a departure date (not just availability) create the most specific urgency — 'depart March 14' is more compelling than 'travel this spring'.
Prompt 14 of 15
Referral thank-you + incentive email
Write a thank-you email to client [NAME] who referred [NEW CLIENT'S FIRST NAME] to [AGENCY NAME]. Express genuine appreciation, mention the referral program reward they've earned ([OFFER — travel credit, gift, upgrade]), and let them know you'll take great care of their referral. Under 100 words. Warm, specific.
💡 Pro tip: Travel referral programs with travel-credit rewards have 2x higher referral rates than cash rewards — travel clients want more travel, not cash.
Prompt 15 of 15
Google Business / Yelp profile description
Write a business description for [AGENCY NAME] travel agency for Google Business or Yelp. Cover: types of travel you specialize in, geographic focus if any, what makes you different from online booking tools, how clients describe working with you (1 testimonial paraphrase), and how to get in touch for a free consultation. Under 200 words. Keyword-rich: '[CITY] travel agent' naturally included.
💡 Pro tip: Travel agency profiles that describe the planning experience ('we handle everything so you arrive without a single spreadsheet') attract the highest-value luxury clients.

15 ChatGPT Prompts for Personal Branding That Build Real Authority

A strong personal brand creates inbound opportunities — jobs, clients, speaking, partnerships — without you having to chase them. These prompts help you define your brand, write your story, and create the content that builds authority over time.

Prompt 1 of 15
LinkedIn 'About' section (summary)
Write a LinkedIn 'About' section for [NAME] who is a [TITLE/ROLE] with [X] years of experience in [FIELD/NICHE]. Their superpower: [SPECIFIC SKILL/EXPERTISE]. Their audience on LinkedIn: [WHO FOLLOWS THEM]. Key achievements: [2-3 ACHIEVEMENTS]. Write in first person. Start with a hook (not 'I am'). End with what kind of people should connect with you. Under 300 words.
💡 Pro tip: LinkedIn About sections that start with a hook (a bold statement, a question, or a counterintuitive insight) are read 5x more than those starting with 'I am a...'
Prompt 2 of 15
Twitter/X bio (punchy, keyword-rich)
Write 5 Twitter/X bio variations for [NAME] who [DOES WHAT] for [AUDIENCE]. Each bio must be under 160 characters. Include: role/niche, one specific outcome or value you create, and optionally a personal detail. Variations should range from professional to personality-forward.
💡 Pro tip: The best Twitter bios are specific enough that the right people immediately know they should follow you.
Prompt 3 of 15
Speaker bio (conference intro)
Write a professional speaker bio for [NAME] for use at [EVENT TYPE — business conference, TEDx, podcast intro]. Include: current role, area of expertise, a notable credential or achievement, who they typically speak to and about, and an engaging closing line. Two versions: 50 words (for emcee intro) and 150 words (for program).
💡 Pro tip: Speaker bios that include one surprising or counterintuitive fact about you are remembered longer by the audience.
Prompt 4 of 15
Positioning statement (personal brand)
Write a personal brand positioning statement for [NAME]. They help [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE] who struggle with [PROBLEM] to achieve [OUTCOME] through [METHOD/APPROACH]. Generate 3 versions: (1) professional/formal, (2) casual/conversational, (3) bold/provocative. Include one that could double as a tagline for their website or social bio.
💡 Pro tip: The positioning statement is the foundation of all your personal brand content. Every post, bio, and pitch should map back to this.
Prompt 5 of 15
Origin story (professional narrative)
Write a professional origin story for [NAME] in [FIELD]. Their background: [BRIEF BACKGROUND]. A pivotal moment or turning point: [MOMENT]. What they learned or decided: [INSIGHT]. Where they are now and what they're building: [CURRENT WORK]. 200-300 words. Narrative arc: problem → struggle → turning point → resolution. Authentic, not self-promotional.
💡 Pro tip: Origin stories that include genuine struggle (not just linear success) build more trust and relatability than polished career summaries.
Prompt 6 of 15
Content strategy (3-month personal brand plan)
Build a 3-month personal brand content strategy for [NAME] on [PRIMARY PLATFORM — LinkedIn/Twitter/Instagram]. Their goal: [GOAL — get speaking gigs/attract clients/get hired]. Audience: [AUDIENCE]. Suggest: 4 content pillars with 3 post ideas each, posting frequency, and the one type of content that will grow their following fastest in their niche. Month-by-month focus.
💡 Pro tip: Personal brand content strategies fail when they're too broad. One platform, one audience, one clear goal for 90 days compounds better than scattered efforts.
Prompt 7 of 15
Podcast pitch (to appear as guest)
Write a pitch email to podcast host [HOST NAME] of '[PODCAST NAME]' proposing [YOUR NAME] as a guest. Lead with a specific reason you're relevant to their audience (reference a recent episode). Pitch 3 specific topics with 1-line descriptions. Include social proof and audience size. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Podcast pitches that reference a specific episode show you're a real listener — this alone doubles response rates.
Prompt 8 of 15
Newsletter launch announcement post
Write a social media post announcing the launch of [NAME]'s newsletter on [TOPIC/NICHE]. Cover: who it's for, what they'll get each issue (specific topics), frequency, and why now. Include a link to subscribe. Under 200 words. Should make the right people immediately want to subscribe.
💡 Pro tip: Newsletter launch posts that describe the specific topics (not just 'tips and insights') attract the right subscribers from day one.
Prompt 9 of 15
Media / press page bio (website)
Write a media page bio for [NAME], suitable for press inquiries, podcast hosts, and conference organizers. Include: who they are (role + niche), their primary area of expertise (1-2 sentences), 3 notable accomplishments or credentials, topics they speak or write about, and contact information for media inquiries. Under 300 words. Third person.
💡 Pro tip: A media page cuts the back-and-forth with hosts and journalists — they can self-serve everything they need to book you.
Prompt 10 of 15
Guest post pitch (to industry blog)
Write a pitch email to the editor of [PUBLICATION/BLOG NAME] proposing a guest article. Include: a specific article idea with a working title, why it's relevant to their audience (reference a recent article they published), your credentials to write it (brief), and your estimated word count and timeline. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Pitches that include a specific, ready-to-go title and angle get accepted at 5x the rate of vague 'I'd love to contribute' pitches.
Prompt 11 of 15
LinkedIn recommendation (to give a colleague)
Write a LinkedIn recommendation for [COLLEAGUE NAME] who is a [ROLE/TITLE]. I've worked with them on [CONTEXT]. Their standout quality: [SPECIFIC QUALITY]. One specific situation where I saw this quality in action: [BRIEF STORY]. Who I would recommend them to: [TYPE OF PERSON/COMPANY]. Under 150 words. Specific, genuine, not generic.
💡 Pro tip: Giving thoughtful recommendations builds reciprocal goodwill — the person you recommend is 4x more likely to recommend you.
Prompt 12 of 15
Signature talk description (speaking kit)
Write a 150-word description of [NAME]'s signature talk titled '[TALK TITLE]'. Cover: who it's for, the core premise or big idea, what the audience will leave with (3 specific takeaways), and the emotional arc of the talk (inspiring, challenging, practical). For use in speaking kit and event organizer pitches.
💡 Pro tip: Talk descriptions that promise 3 specific takeaways (not just 'inspiration') get booked more often by conference organizers.
Prompt 13 of 15
Cold email (partnership / collaboration pitch)
Write a cold email to [NAME] at [COMPANY/BRAND] proposing a collaboration. My platform: [YOUR PLATFORM — podcast, newsletter, community, social]. Their audience: [THEIR AUDIENCE]. The collaboration idea: [SPECIFIC IDEA — joint webinar, newsletter swap, co-created content]. Why it's mutually beneficial: [VALUE TO BOTH]. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Collaboration pitches with a specific format and mutual benefit clearly spelled out convert at 3x the rate of vague 'let's work together' emails.
Prompt 14 of 15
Book proposal executive summary (nonfiction)
Write the executive summary section of a nonfiction book proposal for [BOOK TITLE] by [NAME]. Cover: the book's central argument in 2 sentences, the specific audience it serves, why now is the right moment for this book, and what makes the author uniquely qualified to write it. Under 250 words. Persuasive, agent-ready.
💡 Pro tip: The executive summary is the first thing an agent reads. If it doesn't hook them in 2 sentences, the rest doesn't matter.
Prompt 15 of 15
Keynote intro email (to event organizer)
Write an email to event organizer [NAME] at [CONFERENCE/EVENT] expressing interest in speaking. Include: a brief intro (who you are, your niche), one relevant talk topic with a 2-sentence pitch, relevant speaking experience (2-3 mentions), your audience fit (explain why your content suits their attendees), and a CTA to hop on a call. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Include one line about why your content specifically fits their attendees' challenges — this one sentence determines whether you get a reply.

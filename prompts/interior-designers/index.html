15 ChatGPT Prompts for Interior Designers That Win Premium Projects

Interior design is a visual business that runs on words: proposals, client questionnaires, project briefs, and the social content that attracts ideal clients. These prompts handle the writing so you can stay in your creative zone.

Prompt 1 of 15
Project proposal (residential)
Write a project proposal for a [ROOM TYPE] renovation/design project for [CLIENT NAME]. Include: project scope summary, your design approach and aesthetic direction, deliverables (mood board, space plan, purchasing support, etc.), timeline (phases), investment range [PRICE RANGE], and a CTA. Under 400 words. Professional, creative, aspirational.
💡 Pro tip: Proposals that describe the intended feeling of the finished space (not just deliverables) win premium clients.
Prompt 2 of 15
Client design questionnaire
Create a design questionnaire for new residential clients starting a [ROOM TYPE] project. Ask about: lifestyle and how the space will be used, aesthetic preferences (with style options), budget range, timeline, pet/child considerations, what they love and hate about their current space, and any must-have or must-avoid elements. 12-15 questions. Clear, conversational.
💡 Pro tip: A thorough questionnaire prevents the mid-project 'this isn't what I pictured' moment — the most expensive design mistake.
Prompt 3 of 15
Instagram caption (project reveal)
Write 3 Instagram caption variations for a completed [ROOM TYPE] project reveal. Version 1: design story (the challenge and how you solved it). Version 2: details-focused (specific materials, colors, sources). Version 3: client-inspired (the brief you were given and how you interpreted it). Each under 200 words. 5 relevant hashtags.
💡 Pro tip: Project reveal posts with design story captions (challenge → solution) outperform inspiration posts in saves and follows.
Prompt 4 of 15
Portfolio project description (website)
Write a portfolio project description for [PROJECT NAME] on an interior design website. Include: the client brief in one sentence, the design challenge, your creative approach, key materials/finishes/sources, and the outcome. 150-200 words. Professional, evocative.
💡 Pro tip: Portfolio descriptions that explain the brief and your decision-making process convert browsers to inquirers better than just listing finishes.
Prompt 5 of 15
Trade partner email (to supplier/manufacturer)
Write an email to [SUPPLIER/MANUFACTURER NAME] introducing myself as an interior designer based in [CITY] and requesting trade account access. Include: a brief bio (years of experience, project types, annual purchasing volume estimate), the type of projects I work on, and a request for their trade application and any showroom appointments. Under 200 words. Professional.
💡 Pro tip: Trade accounts unlock 15-40% discounts. Every supplier relationship you build early pays dividends for decades.
Prompt 6 of 15
Consultation follow-up email
Write a follow-up email to [PROSPECT NAME] after an initial design consultation for their [PROJECT TYPE]. Reference 2-3 specific things they mentioned wanting ([SPECIFIC NEEDS]). Outline the proposed next steps, include a brief summary of your proposed approach, and provide a link or attachment to your proposal or scope. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Follow-up emails that reference specific client desires (using their exact words when possible) show that you listened — the #1 client concern with designers.
Prompt 7 of 15
Website 'About' page (designer bio)
Write an About page for interior designer [NAME] based in [CITY] specializing in [STYLE/NICHE — e.g., modern residential, boutique hospitality]. Include: design philosophy, how you work with clients, a personal story that reveals your aesthetic sensibility, and a CTA to start a conversation. Under 300 words. Confident, warm, memorable.
💡 Pro tip: The best designer About pages make the reader feel understood ('she designs for the way people actually live') — not just impressed.
Prompt 8 of 15
Houzz / design platform profile bio
Write a profile bio for [NAME] interior design studio on Houzz or similar platform. Cover: specialty and style, location and service area, types of projects taken, design process overview (1-2 sentences), and what clients say about working with you (1 paraphrased testimonial). Under 200 words. Keyword-rich for the platform.
💡 Pro tip: Houzz profiles with a clear niche ('we specialize in complete residential renovations for families in [city]') attract better inquiries than generalists.
Prompt 9 of 15
Cold outreach to developer/builder
Write a cold email to [DEVELOPER/BUILDER NAME] proposing a design partnership for their [PROJECT TYPE — luxury spec homes, commercial fit-outs, model units]. Reference a specific project they've completed or are building. Explain how a design partnership benefits them (faster sales, better photography, higher perceived value). Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Developer partnerships are the highest-leverage client relationships in residential design — one relationship can generate 10+ projects per year.
Prompt 10 of 15
Change order explanation email
Write an email to client [NAME] explaining a change order for their [PROJECT TYPE] project. The change is [DESCRIPTION]. The additional cost is [AMOUNT]. Explain why it's needed (outside original scope or unforeseen condition), what it adds, and what happens if they decline. Professional, direct, no apologizing for charging for the work. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: Change orders explained proactively (before starting the extra work) are accepted at 90% the rate. After the fact, it drops to 50%.
Prompt 11 of 15
Blog post (design tips for target client)
Write a 400-word blog post for [DESIGNER NAME]'s website titled '[BLOG TITLE — e.g., 5 Things to Consider Before Starting a Kitchen Renovation]'. Target audience: [HOMEOWNER TYPE]. Include: 5 practical tips with brief explanations, 1 designer insight per tip, and a CTA to book a consultation at the end. SEO keyword: '[KEYWORD]'.
💡 Pro tip: Blog posts that address renovation decision-making ('what to consider before starting') attract clients who are 3-6 months away from hiring — the best planning window.
Prompt 12 of 15
Project kickoff email (new client)
Write a project kickoff email to new client [NAME] beginning their [PROJECT TYPE]. Include: excitement about working together, summary of what's happening in Phase 1, what you need from them (access, decisions, payments), timeline for first deliverable, and communication preferences. Under 250 words. Warm, organized, confidence-building.
💡 Pro tip: A clear kickoff email eliminates the most common client anxiety: 'I don't know what happens next or what I need to do.'
Prompt 13 of 15
Testimonial request email (end of project)
Write an email to client [NAME] requesting a testimonial at the end of their completed project. Provide 3 optional reflection questions: (1) What was the design challenge? (2) What was your experience working with me? (3) How does the finished space make you feel? Include where they can post it (Google, Houzz, Instagram tag). Under 100 words.
💡 Pro tip: Interior design testimonials that describe the feeling of the finished space ('I finally love coming home') convert browsers to inquiries better than service descriptions.
Prompt 14 of 15
Referral thank-you note (to referring client)
Write a thank-you note to client [NAME] who referred [NEW CLIENT'S FIRST NAME] to me. Express genuine appreciation, mention that referrals are the highest compliment, and include a small gesture of thanks ([GIFT CARD / PERSONAL NOTE / FLOWERS depending on your style]). Under 100 words. Personal, not corporate.
💡 Pro tip: A tangible thank-you for referrals (not just an email) doubles the likelihood of that client referring again.
Prompt 15 of 15
E-design service landing page
Write landing page copy for an e-design (virtual interior design) service offered by [DESIGNER NAME]. Include: who it's for (clients outside your area, budget-conscious renovators), what they get (mood board, space plan, shopping list, etc.), how it works (3-4 steps), price ([AMOUNT]), and a CTA. Under 400 words. Accessible, professional.
💡 Pro tip: E-design services extend your geographic reach infinitely and are 80% lower effort per project. The landing page is the only marketing tool you need.

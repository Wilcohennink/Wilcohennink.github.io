15 ChatGPT Prompts for Insurance Agents That Build Relationships and Win Referrals

Insurance is a trust business built on relationships, education, and timely follow-up. These prompts help you communicate clearly with prospects and clients at every stage — without spending hours writing from scratch.

Prompt 1 of 15
Coverage needs analysis email (prospect)
Write an email to prospect [NAME] following up after discussing their insurance needs. Summarize what we discussed: their current coverage ([CURRENT COVERAGE]), gaps identified ([GAPS]), and the solutions I'm recommending ([SOLUTIONS]). Invite them to a 20-minute follow-up call to review the quotes. Under 200 words. Educational, not pushy.
💡 Pro tip: Emails that summarize the conversation and identify specific gaps feel like advice, not sales — conversion rates are 3x higher.
Prompt 2 of 15
Cold outreach (small business owner)
Write a cold email to [BUSINESS OWNER NAME] at [BUSINESS TYPE] in [CITY] about their business insurance. Lead with a common coverage gap in their industry ([GAP]). Offer a free policy review. Under 150 words. Helpful framing — not a pitch.
💡 Pro tip: Industry-specific coverage gap emails feel like a warning, not a sales pitch. Small business owners respond at 3x the rate of generic outreach.
Prompt 3 of 15
Policy renewal reminder email
Write a policy renewal reminder email to client [NAME] whose [POLICY TYPE] renews on [DATE]. Include: renewal date, any changes to their premium or coverage ([IF KNOWN]), a prompt to review whether their coverage still matches their life/business situation, and a CTA to schedule a brief review call. Under 150 words. Proactive, helpful.
💡 Pro tip: Renewal reminders sent 60 days before expiry have the highest retention rate. Clients who feel informed don't shop around.
Prompt 4 of 15
LinkedIn post (coverage myth-buster)
Write a LinkedIn post busting a common myth about [TYPE OF INSURANCE — e.g., renters insurance, life insurance, business liability]. Hook: challenge the misconception in the first line. Body: 3 paragraphs explaining the reality with a relatable example. Takeaway: what readers should do. Under 1,200 characters. End with a soft CTA to DM for a free review.
💡 Pro tip: Myth-busting posts generate the highest shares in insurance content — people tag friends who believe the myth.
Prompt 5 of 15
Life insurance needs analysis explainer
Write a client-friendly explainer email about how to determine the right amount of life insurance coverage. Cover: the DIME method (Debt, Income, Mortgage, Education) in plain English, a worked example for someone earning [INCOME] with [MORTGAGE] and [DEPENDENTS], and an invitation to run a personalized analysis together. Under 250 words. Add a disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Clients who understand how their coverage amount was calculated renew and refer at higher rates than those who were just sold a number.
Prompt 6 of 15
Referral request email (existing client)
Write an email to happy client [NAME] asking for a referral. Express appreciation for the relationship, briefly mention the types of clients I help most ([CLIENT PROFILE]), and make a specific ask: 'If you know a [BUSINESS OWNER / FAMILY WITH YOUNG KIDS / etc.] who might benefit from a free policy review, I'd welcome the introduction.' Under 120 words.
💡 Pro tip: Referral requests tied to a specific client profile ('do you know a small business owner') convert at 4x the rate of vague asks.
Prompt 7 of 15
Claims support follow-up email
Write a follow-up email to client [NAME] who recently filed a claim for [CLAIM TYPE]. Check in on their experience, offer to answer any questions about the process, and reaffirm your commitment to supporting them through resolution. Under 150 words. Empathetic, practical, relationship-strengthening.
💡 Pro tip: Claims are the moments that determine whether clients renew and refer. A single proactive call or email during claims doubles referral rates.
Prompt 8 of 15
Coverage explainer (homeowner's insurance)
Write a plain-English explainer on what homeowner's insurance does and doesn't cover, targeted at first-time homebuyers. Cover: what's typically included (dwelling, liability, personal property), 3 common items NOT covered (flood, earthquake, home business), and when an umbrella policy makes sense. Under 300 words. Add a disclaimer.
💡 Pro tip: Evergreen explainer content on insurance basics is one of the top-ranking content types for local insurance agent SEO.
Prompt 9 of 15
Facebook/Instagram ad (free review offer)
Write a Facebook/Instagram ad for a free insurance review for [TARGET AUDIENCE — e.g., small business owners in [CITY], homeowners over 40]. Include: a hook about a common coverage gap or overpayment, what the free review includes, and a CTA (DM, call, or landing page link). 3 variations. Under 125 words each.
💡 Pro tip: Free review ads that quantify potential savings ('most clients find they're over-insured or under-insured by X') dramatically outperform generic offers.
Prompt 10 of 15
Cross-sell email (auto to home, life to disability)
Write a cross-sell email to existing client [NAME] who currently has [CURRENT POLICY TYPE] with me. Introduce the idea of also insuring their [NEW POLICY TYPE]. Lead with a benefit (convenience of one agent, potential multi-policy discount). Keep it low-pressure — open the conversation, don't close it in one email. Under 150 words.
💡 Pro tip: Clients with 2+ policies have 5x lower churn than single-policy clients. Every cross-sell conversation is worth having.
Prompt 11 of 15
New client welcome email
Write a welcome email to new client [NAME] who just purchased a [POLICY TYPE] policy. Include: confirmation that coverage is in force, key policy details ([COVERAGE AMOUNT, EFFECTIVE DATE]), what to do in case they need to file a claim, and how to reach me. Warm, organized, reassuring. Under 200 words.
💡 Pro tip: New client welcome emails that explain the claims process reduce panic and confusion when clients actually need to file.
Prompt 12 of 15
Newsletter (quarterly client update)
Write a quarterly newsletter section for insurance clients covering: one recent change in [INSURANCE REGULATION/MARKET CONDITION] that might affect their coverage, one seasonal tip ([SEASON-SPECIFIC — e.g., 'update your home inventory before hurricane season'], and a brief note from me. Under 200 words. Educational, not sales-y.
💡 Pro tip: Insurance agents who send quarterly educational newsletters retain clients 40% longer than those with no communication between renewals.
Prompt 13 of 15
Objection handler: 'I already have insurance'
Write a response to a prospect who says 'I already have insurance.' Acknowledge it positively, pivot to a relevant question about their current coverage ('[COVERAGE GAP QUESTION]'), and offer a free, no-obligation comparison. Do not badmouth their current carrier. Under 150 words. Helpful, confident.
💡 Pro tip: The goal of this response is not to get them to switch — it's to get them to agree to a review. That's when they find the gap.
Prompt 14 of 15
Website bio (insurance agent)
Write a professional bio for insurance agent [NAME] at [AGENCY] specializing in [LINES OF COVERAGE] in [CITY/REGION]. Include: years of experience, specialties, a human detail that builds trust, carriers represented (if applicable), and how to get a free quote or review. Under 200 words. First or third person (ask agent preference).
💡 Pro tip: Bios that mention specific client types you help ('I specialize in coverage for growing families and small businesses') attract better-fit inquiries.
Prompt 15 of 15
Google review request (post-renewal)
Write an email to client [NAME] asking for a Google review after their smooth [POLICY TYPE] renewal. Keep it brief: thank them, explain the value of reviews for helping others make informed choices, and include the direct Google review link. Under 75 words. No pressure, just a warm ask.
💡 Pro tip: Post-renewal is the perfect moment to request a review — clients just said yes to you again, satisfaction is at a peak.
